# Mixed Particle Claim Packet

This note records the current DASHI handling for social/news packets that
collapse collider hadron spectroscopy and low-dimensional anyon statistics
into one "new particles" claim.

The packet is split into three receipts:

- `XiCCPlus`: the 2026 CERN/LHCb result is one new doubly charmed baryon,
  quark content `c c d`, observed in proton-proton collision reconstruction.
  It is a QCD/hadron-spectrum datum, not a new force or Standard Model rewrite.
- `LHCbExoticHadrons2022`: the "three exotic particles" phrasing belongs to
  the 2022 LHCb outreach result: a strange pentaquark, a doubly charged
  tetraquark, and a neutral tetraquark partner.  These are exotic hadron
  candidates/states, not elementary-particle promotion by default.
- `OneDAnyon`: the 2026 OIST/University of Oklahoma lane is not a CERN/LHC
  discovery.  It concerns one-dimensional anyon theory, tunable exchange
  statistics, and possible experimental routes in low-dimensional quantum
  systems.

The formal boundary is:

```text
LHCb hadrons -> binding grammar / QCD spectroscopy
Anyons       -> exchange grammar / low-dimensional statistics
```

Supported promotions are narrow: hadron spectrum extension, QCD constraint,
exotic binding evidence, and low-dimensional tunable exchange statistics.

Forbidden promotions remain fail-closed: new fundamental force, Standard Model
rewrite, elementary-particle promotion from a hadron state, "three particles
today" for the 2026 LHCb result, and "LHC anyon discovery".
