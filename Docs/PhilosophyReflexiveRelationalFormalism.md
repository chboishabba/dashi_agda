# Reflexive Relational Philosophy Formalism

## Scope

This hierarchy formalises a philosophy-side DASHI surface without promoting philosophical mappings into mathematical, clinical, political, or cultural authority.

The central second-order move is:

```text
x -> (x, frame(x))
```

`1.0` knowledge is immersed experience. `1.1` knowledge represents the experience together with the frame, observer position, residuals, and possible interventions. This is reflected situatedness, not a view from nowhere.

## Core sequence

```text
experience
-> reflexive doubling
-> local star normalisation
-> inter-chart translation
-> relational atlas
-> collective organisation
-> material intervention
-> changed feedback
```

The local chart provides self-orientation:

```text
N_star(x) = star^-1 * x
N_star(star) = 1
```

The operations are supplied by a typed `LocalUnitStructure`; no arbitrary philosophical carrier is asserted to be a field.

Globally, `star` is read through the symbolic index `10`. The relational extension has index `11 = 10 + 1`. The extra structure is an explicit other position and relation, not a theorem that arithmetic creates social relation.

## Philosophical commitments

The formal surface records:

- process ontology: possibility, cut, relation, provisional world, marked extension, relational extension;
- situated state: observation, relation, commitment, history, action, perspective, constraint, authority, residual, and exterior;
- encounter rather than guaranteed synthesis;
- transformation without unrecorded erasure;
- Tlurey as a trust-, care-, challenge-, and repair-indexed relation;
- Respect -> Connect -> Reflect -> Direct as a typed protocol;
- multiple context valuations without arbitrary equivalence;
- distress as a non-unique situated mismatch signal;
- teaching as pattern-preserving participation rather than total cultural representation;
- material politics as intervention in reproduction feedback;
- plural attachment without false equality of history, power, injury, responsibility, or political status;
- decentring the sovereign individual without granting impersonal systems moral sovereignty.

## Promotion boundary

The canonical surface is fail-closed. It carries no tokens for:

- numerological authority;
- clinical authority;
- political authority;
- Indigenous representation authority;
- Moonshine-to-social theorem promotion;
- observer-alone topology change;
- equal-claim inference from plural attachment;
- moral sovereignty for inhuman or machinic systems.

Actual topology change requires organisation, resources, access or authority, safety, time, coordination, and an effective operator. Reflexivity begins the route to leverage; it does not complete it.
