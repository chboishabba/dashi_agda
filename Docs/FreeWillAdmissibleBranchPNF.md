# Free Will Admissible Branch PNF

`DASHI/Reasoning/FreeWillAdmissibleBranchPNF.agda` records the thread delta
for free-will language as a bounded reasoning surface:

- free will is represented only as observer-local navigation through nearby
  admissible manifold branches;
- PNF evidence and population packets can be fused, but the fusion remains
  residual-bearing;
- conceivable alternatives are observer-local estimates of admissible branch
  volume, not complete enumerations;
- choice and world constraints are inverse/bidirectional and must carry
  receipts/residuals.

The canonical toy inhabitant uses a situated observer, a toy world, a nearby
branch, an alternate branch, a local choice, a population packet, and a finite
nearby branch-volume estimate.  Its fusion residual is held at
`noTypedMeet`, while the bidirectional constraint residual is only `partial`.

## Non-Claims

The canonical guard list blocks:

- metaphysical free-will proof;
- determinism proof;
- individual intent recovery;
- population consensus as truth;
- arbitrary fantasy promotion;
- complete branch enumeration.

The module is therefore a typed vocabulary and non-promotion certificate, not a
proof of free will, determinism, intent, truth by population, or exhaustive
world-branch search.
