# 6. Conclusions

The central result of this paper is narrow and deliberately non-promoting.
The carrier programme now has a coherent `p=7` three-generation architecture:
the CM smooth-point calculation and the Hurwitz uniqueness calculation both
select the same lane, and the Klein quartic supplies
\[
J(K) \simeq X_0(49)^3
\]
with CM by \(\mathcal O_{-7}\) on each factor.  The order-seven character of
the relevant \(PSL(2,\mathbb F_7)\) representation recovers the CM period
\(\tau_{-7}=(-1+i\sqrt7)/2\).  This is a genuine object-level structure, not a
fit to CKM data.

The auxiliary p-adic and lineage observations should be read in that same
limited way.  The `p=7` lane has a structural convergence reading: its six
nonzero directions pair into three sign-opposite direction classes, matching
the three-slot carrier architecture without deriving the mixing data.  The p-adic
carrier coordinate separates the integer projection from the residual decimal
defect: the integer part maps through \(\mathbb Z\to\mathbb Z_p\), while the
fractional defect remains in \(C\)-space.  The ternary attractor
\(-1/2=1+3+3^2+\cdots\) matches
\(\operatorname{Re}(\tau_{-7})\), and the braid path through \(B_3\) and
Yang-Baxter consistency meets the ternary/3-adic path at the Klein quartic.
These are origin and consistency checks, not additional CKM promotions.
The corresponding \(p=7\) Structural Convergence Remark should be read the
same way: \(p=7\) appears spectrally as the smallest finite product gap,
dynamically as the deepest generation-prime depth below physical viscous
cutoffs, geometrically through the \(X_0(49)\) / Bruhat-Tits level,
algebraically through the full \(PSL(2,\mathbb F_7)\) bilinear no-go,
arithmetically through the CM period, through the 7-adic phase split
\(7\equiv 1\pmod 3\), and historically through Hurwitz uniqueness.  No other
prime satisfies all seven characterisations, but independence of the seven
characterisations has not been proved; this is a structural convergence
remark, not a convergence theorem.  The new helical diagram chase uses
\(\mathbb Z/3\mathbb Z=\{1,2,4\}\subset\mathbb F_7^\times\), generated by
2, with \(\psi_k(2)=\omega^k\).  Since \(\omega\in\mathbb Z_7\), the
idempotents split over \(\mathbb Z_7\), and
\[
T_7(X_0(49))\otimes_{\mathbb Z_7}\mathbb Z_7[\mathbb Z/3\mathbb Z]
\simeq T_7(X_0(49))^3 .
\]
This proves the arithmetic splitting and the three-character distinction.
The branch convention with the existing single-factor morphism \(\phi\) is
corrected: \(\phi\) matches the \(\psi_2\) branch via
\(\mathrm{Frob}_{p2}=4=2^2\), so the stale zero-branch commutativity
language is superseded.  This is structural evidence and branch bookkeeping
for the chosen carrier architecture, not a physical mixing theorem.

It is not yet a CKM matrix.  The three factors have a proved arithmetic
three-character split, but canonical helical labelling still requires the
\(\psi_2/\phi\) branch convention to be propagated and the concrete morphism
skeleton integrated with the CKM receipts.  The physical mixing angles require
the full down-type Yukawa structure \(Y_d\).  The corrected
\(PSL(2,\mathbb F_7)\) texture constraint sharpens this boundary rather than
closing it: the relevant tensor product is
\[
V_3\otimes V_3 = V_3'\oplus V_6,
\]
with no trivial representation.  Therefore the down-type Yukawa sector still
needs a symmetry-breaking input before \(Y_d\) can be promoted.  The minimal
recorded frontier is a \(V_3\) spurion: the triple tensor
\(V_3\otimes V_3\otimes V_3\) has one verified invariant, but it is the
exterior determinant channel.  A pure \(V_3\) spurion therefore gives a
generic antisymmetric rank-two texture.  The rank-one heavy-generation matrix
requires an additional residual selector and is not forced by
\(PSL(2,\mathbb F_7)\).  A \(V_6\) spurion is a wider breaking route, not a
derived matrix.  The direct shortcut
\(\gamma=\arctan(\sqrt7)\) is retired: it gives \(69.30^\circ\), in
\(5.4\sigma\) tension with the HFLAV value \(65.5\pm0.7^\circ\), and the finite
7-isogeny audit supplies no correction mechanism.  Thus \(|V_{cb}|\),
\(\gamma\), \(\beta\), \(\alpha\), \(\rho\), and \(\eta\) remain open from the
carrier side.  \(\lambda\) remains a Georgi-Jarlskog compatibility, and
\(|V_{ub}|\approx\alpha_1\alpha_2\) remains only a leading-order structural
hypothesis in the \(U_d\approx I\) approximation.

The new depth-grading idea belongs at this same frontier.  The rule
\[
\operatorname{depth}(p)=\lfloor\log_2(p)\rfloor
\]
is a candidate way to grade prime carriers for residual Yukawa selection.  It
places \(p=7\) at depth \(2\), but this is only a selector hypothesis.  It
does not establish the rank-one heavy-generation projector, does not produce
\(Y_d\), and cannot be used for canonical CKM labelling before the
\(\psi_2/\phi\) branch convention and remaining morphism work are in place.

The Navier-Stokes part of the paper gives a different kind of result.  Under
the Littlewood-Paley identification of carrier depth with dyadic wavenumber
bands, a Kolmogorov spectrum \(E(K)\sim K^{-5/3}\) gives band energy
\(\|u_k\|^2\sim 2^{-2k/3}\), so the natural carrier decay is
\(\alpha_K=2^{-2/3}\).  The corresponding standard \(H^{11/8}\) contribution
is
\[
2^{11k/4}2^{-2k/3}=2^{25k/12},
\]
which diverges.  Finite active depth therefore gives a route to standard
\(H^{11/8}\) and Serrin regularity, while unbounded depth is exactly where
that route can fail.  The viscous-tail calculation adds the threshold
\[
K^*(\nu)=\frac34\log_2(1/\nu),
\]
but only under Kolmogorov calibration.  This does not prove blow-up and does
not prove Clay; it localises the carrier transfer problem to depth control,
tail dominance, and norm comparison.  The current MDL bridge receipt records
this as a candidate only: active-depth/tail control for the actual
Navier-Stokes evolution is still the missing forward estimate.  The estimate
must control the cumulative tail, not just an isolated dyadic band: above
\(K^*(\nu)\), the active-depth contributions have to be summed for the actual
flow and compared to the standard Sobolev/Serrin norm.

FRACTRAN admissibility sharpens this only on the carrier side: it is a
nonresonance condition for admissible algebraic tails and NS tail dominance,
not a transfer theorem for the actual flow.

The adelic route is therefore not a generic citation to p-adic analysis.  The
remaining candidate path is automorphic: use a \(GL(1)\) / Hecke-character
comparison to relate adelic carrier norms to archimedean Sobolev control.
The existing literature checks do not provide this theorem, so it remains a
new proof obligation and gives no NS, Clay, or unification promotion.

The finite spectral-gap evidence is also bounded.  On the finite
\(\mathbb Z/7\) carrier, the unnormalised cycle gap is
\[
2-2\cos(2\pi/7)\approx0.753.
\]
For the normalised random-walk Laplacian the gap is half this value.
For the product carrier \(\mathbb Z/7\times\mathbb Z/3\times\mathbb Z/2\), the
factor gaps are \(0.753\), \(3\), and \(2\), so the \(\mathbb Z/7\) factor
continues to set the finite bottleneck.
This is a finite calculation, not a continuum Yang-Mills mass gap.  It may be
kept as evidence for the arithmetic carrier ledger, but it cannot be elevated
to a Clay claim from the present sources.  The next finite spectral target is
the actual Hecke operator on \(S_2(X_0(49))\).  The checked table through
\(p=19\) is
\[
a_2=1,\quad a_3=0,\quad a_5=0,\quad a_7=0,\quad a_{11}=4,\quad
a_{13}=a_{17}=a_{19}=0.
\]
The \(p=7\) entry is separated as the ramified level prime.  Since the usual
trivial-character cusp space is one-dimensional, \(T_p\) acts by a scalar and
there is no intrinsic multi-eigenvalue Hecke gap computed here.

The remaining mathematics is precise.  For NS, one needs a usable
depth-control theorem, a cumulative tail estimate for the actual flow, or a
new automorphic \(GL(1)\) / Hecke-character adelic Sobolev comparison theorem.
For CKM, one needs the corrected \(\psi_2/\phi\) convention
\(\mathrm{Frob}_{p2}=4=2^2\) propagated through the existing single-factor
morphism, the three-factor morphism into \(T_7(X_0(49))^3\),
verification of the \(V_3\)-spurion exterior channel, a residual selector if
rank one is desired, and then the full down-type Yukawa dynamics with symmetry
breaking.  For \(p=7\), the current independence criterion supports only a
structural convergence remark: no checked prime other than \(7\) satisfies the
seven readings, but full logical independence is unproved.  For finite
spectral evidence, one needs a meaningful finite cusp spectral diagnostic
beyond scalar \(T_p\) action before discussing any continuum bridge.  For YM,
the carrier has only a continuum cusp-to-flat equivalence; the mass gap
remains the flat four-dimensional Yang-Mills Clay problem.  These are
distinct kinds of hardness and the paper keeps them separate.
