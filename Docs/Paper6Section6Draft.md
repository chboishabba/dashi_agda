# 6. Conclusions

The central result of this paper is narrow but non-trivial.  Within the
Heegner-CM carrier conventions used here, arithmetic geometry supplies two
Wolfenstein-sector quantities at sub-percent comparison precision without
fitting them to CKM data:

\[
\lambda_{\mathrm{carrier}}
= \alpha_1 \pi\sqrt{3}
\simeq 0.224403,
\]

and

\[
|V_{cb}|_{\mathrm{carrier}}
= \alpha_1^2 \cdot 14 \cdot \frac{4\pi}{3\sqrt{6}}
\simeq 0.04072.
\]

The first value is the Cabibbo/Wolfenstein-\(\lambda\) diagnostic from the
discriminant \(-3\) lane.  The second is the \(2\)-\(3\) mixing diagnostic from
the \(-3\), \(-7\) lane interaction, using the inert \(T_{13}\) Hecke
correspondence on the \(\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{21})\) Hilbert-modular surface and the
stated modular-volume normalization.  In both cases the numerical comparison
is made after the carrier value has been fixed by the arithmetic construction;
the observed CKM values are not used to choose the constants.

This is not a derivation of the full CKM matrix.  The present construction does
not derive the Wolfenstein \(\rho\) and \(\eta\) coordinates, does not derive
\(|V_{ub}|\), and does not derive the physical CP-violating phase
\(\delta_{\mathrm{CP}}\).  Candidate structures for the \(1\)-\(3\) entry and
for CP phase generation are useful diagnostics, but they remain exploratory
until their normalisation, embedding choice, and physical authority are fixed
independently.

The appropriate conclusion is therefore that the Heegner-CM programme already
captures the leading Wolfenstein hierarchy and the \(V_{cb}\)-scale lane with
unexpected numerical sharpness, while the genuinely three-generation part of
CKM physics is still open.  The next stage of the programme is to identify a
carrier mechanism for CP violation and to explain the third-generation mixing
structure, especially the simultaneous origin of \(|V_{ub}|\), \(\rho\),
\(\eta\), and \(\delta_{\mathrm{CP}}\), without importing them from a
phenomenological fit.
