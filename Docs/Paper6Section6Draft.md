# 6. Conclusions

The central result of this paper is narrow and deliberately non-promoting.
The carrier programme now has a coherent `p=7` three-generation architecture:
the CM smooth-point calculation and the Hurwitz uniqueness calculation both
select the same lane, and the Klein quartic supplies
\[
J(K) \simeq X_0(49)^3
\]
with CM by \(\mathcal O_{-7}\) on each factor.  The order-seven character of
the relevant \(PSL(2,\mathbb F_7)\) representation recovers the CM period
\(\tau_{-7}=(-1+i\sqrt7)/2\).  This is a genuine object-level structure, not a
fit to CKM data.

The auxiliary p-adic and lineage observations should be read in that same
limited way.  The `p=7` lane has a structural bottleneck: its six nonzero
directions pair into three sign-opposite direction classes, matching the
three-slot carrier architecture without deriving the mixing data.  The p-adic
carrier coordinate separates the integer projection from the residual decimal
defect: the integer part maps through \(\mathbb Z\to\mathbb Z_p\), while the
fractional defect remains in \(C\)-space.  The ternary attractor
\(-1/2=1+3+3^2+\cdots\) matches
\(\operatorname{Re}(\tau_{-7})\), and the braid path through \(B_3\) and
Yang-Baxter consistency meets the ternary/3-adic path at the Klein quartic.
These are origin and consistency checks, not additional CKM promotions.
The corresponding bottleneck remark should be read the same way: \(p=7\)
appears spectrally as the smallest finite product gap, dynamically as the
deepest generation-prime depth below physical viscous cutoffs, geometrically
through the \(X_0(49)\) / Bruhat-Tits level, algebraically through the full
\(PSL(2,\mathbb F_7)\) bilinear no-go, arithmetically through the CM period,
and historically through Hurwitz uniqueness.  The agreement is structural
evidence for the chosen carrier architecture, not a physical mixing theorem.

It is not yet a CKM matrix.  The three factors still require a carrier
morphism that labels the generation slots, and the physical mixing angles
require the full down-type Yukawa structure \(Y_d\).  The corrected
\(PSL(2,\mathbb F_7)\) texture constraint sharpens this boundary rather than
closing it: the relevant tensor product is
\[
V_3\otimes V_3 = V_3'\oplus V_6,
\]
with no trivial representation.  Therefore the down-type Yukawa sector still
needs a symmetry-breaking input before \(Y_d\) can be promoted.  The direct shortcut
\(\gamma=\arctan(\sqrt7)\) is retired: it gives \(69.30^\circ\), in
\(5.4\sigma\) tension with the HFLAV value \(65.5\pm0.7^\circ\), and the finite
7-isogeny audit supplies no correction mechanism.  Thus \(|V_{cb}|\),
\(\gamma\), \(\beta\), \(\alpha\), \(\rho\), and \(\eta\) remain open from the
carrier side.  \(\lambda\) remains a Georgi-Jarlskog compatibility, and
\(|V_{ub}|\approx\alpha_1\alpha_2\) remains only a leading-order structural
hypothesis in the \(U_d\approx I\) approximation.

The Navier-Stokes part of the paper gives a different kind of result.  Under
the Littlewood-Paley identification of carrier depth with dyadic wavenumber
bands, a Kolmogorov spectrum \(E(K)\sim K^{-5/3}\) gives band energy
\(\|u_k\|^2\sim 2^{-2k/3}\), so the natural carrier decay is
\(\alpha_K=2^{-2/3}\).  The corresponding standard \(H^{11/8}\) contribution
is
\[
2^{11k/4}2^{-2k/3}=2^{25k/12},
\]
which diverges.  Finite active depth therefore gives a route to standard
\(H^{11/8}\) and Serrin regularity, while unbounded depth is exactly where
that route can fail.  The viscous-tail calculation adds the threshold
\[
K^*(\nu)=\frac34\log_2(1/\nu),
\]
but only under Kolmogorov calibration.  This does not prove blow-up and does
not prove Clay; it localises the carrier transfer problem to depth control,
tail dominance, and norm comparison.

The finite spectral-gap evidence is also bounded.  On the finite
\(\mathbb Z/7\) carrier, the unnormalised cycle gap is
\[
2-2\cos(2\pi/7)\approx0.753.
\]
For the normalised random-walk Laplacian the gap is half this value.
For the product carrier \(\mathbb Z/7\times\mathbb Z/3\times\mathbb Z/2\), the
factor gaps are \(0.753\), \(3\), and \(2\), so the \(\mathbb Z/7\) factor
continues to set the finite bottleneck.
This is a finite calculation, not a continuum Yang-Mills mass gap.  It may be
kept as evidence for the arithmetic carrier ledger, but it cannot be elevated
to a Clay claim from the present sources.

The remaining mathematics is precise.  For NS, one needs a usable depth-control
theorem or a classification of non-trivial depth-closed classes.  For CKM, one
needs the three-factor morphism into \(T_7(X_0(49))^3\) and then the full
down-type Yukawa dynamics with symmetry breaking.  For finite spectral
evidence, one needs a bridge from the finite \(\mathbb Z/7\) calculation to a
continuum operator before any stronger statement is available.  For YM, the
carrier has only a continuum cusp-to-flat equivalence; the mass gap remains
the flat four-dimensional Yang-Mills Clay problem.  These are distinct kinds
of hardness and the paper keeps them separate.
