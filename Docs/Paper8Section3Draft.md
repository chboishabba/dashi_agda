# Section 3: Quark-Lepton Separation

The quark-lepton split used in Paper 8 is an arithmetic separation at the
level of conductors.  The claim in this section is deliberately structural: it
identifies two different conductor roles inside the same multiplicative
bookkeeping.  It is not, by itself, a full derivation of the Standard Model
matter representations, Yukawa sector, mass spectrum, neutrino sector, or
complete electroweak representation theory.

The quark lanes are the three primitive conductor lanes

```text
{ 3, 4, 7 }.
```

Here `3` and `7` are prime conductors, while `4 = 2^2` is a prime-power
conductor.  The three conductors are pairwise coprime:

```text
gcd(3,4) = 1,   gcd(3,7) = 1,   gcd(4,7) = 1.
```

This coprimality is the structural point.  The quark conductors form the
multiplicative basis for the lane system: they are independent conductor
directions, not overlaps assembled from other quark lanes.  In this arithmetic
sense they are the basis lanes against which the lepton lanes are read.

The charged-lepton lanes are the three pairwise multiplicative closure lanes

```text
{ 12, 28, 21 } = { 3 x 4, 4 x 7, 3 x 7 }.
```

Unlike the quark conductors, these conductors are not pairwise-coprime basis
directions.  Each lepton conductor is a product of two quark conductors and
therefore shares factors with exactly two quark lanes:

```text
12 = 3 x 4   shares factors with quark lanes 3 and 4
28 = 4 x 7   shares factors with quark lanes 4 and 7
21 = 3 x 7   shares factors with quark lanes 3 and 7
```

Thus the separation is arithmetic rather than imposed by labels.  Quarks sit
on the pairwise-coprime multiplicative basis conductors `{3,4,7}`.  Charged
leptons sit on the pairwise products generated by closing that basis under
two-factor multiplication.  The lepton conductors are consequently overlap
lanes in the multiplicative closure of the quark basis, not additional
primitive quark-style basis lanes.

The bookkeeping may be recorded as

```text
electron : 12 = 3 x 4
muon     : 21 = 3 x 7
tau      : 28 = 4 x 7
```

These family labels state the proposed correspondence between the three
charged-lepton lanes and the three pairwise products of the quark conductor
basis.  They do not prove the observed charged-lepton masses, weak-isospin
assignments, neutrino mixing data, hypercharge normalization, or the full
Standard Model representation category.

The physical charge structure is expected to follow from this conductor
structure through the Hecke `U(1)_Y` action.  In the present paper this is a
structural candidate statement: the conductor split supplies the arithmetic
support on which the hypercharge action is to be represented.  It is not yet a
complete proof that the full Standard Model charge table has been derived from
the conductor data alone.

The absence of a fourth charged-lepton lane is likewise only a structural
count at this stage.  Three pairwise products are available from three
pairwise-coprime quark basis conductors, so the closure bookkeeping supplies
exactly three charged-lepton candidate lanes.  Promoting this count to a
physical exclusion theorem for a fourth charged-lepton family would require an
additional receipt beyond the arithmetic separation recorded here.

Status: Section 3 establishes the conductor-level quark-lepton separation as a
structural receipt.  Quark lanes are the pairwise-coprime prime or prime-power
basis conductors `{3,4,7}`.  Charged-lepton lanes are the multiplicative
closure conductors `{12,28,21}` obtained as pairwise products of that basis.
The Hecke `U(1)_Y` action is the proposed mechanism by which physical charge
structure follows from the conductor structure, but the claim remains
candidate-level until the full Standard Model representation proof is supplied.
