# 1. Introduction

DASHI is a fail-closed arithmetic programme for recording which pieces of a
carrier-based physics construction are proved, which are diagnostic, and which
have been explicitly retired.  The purpose of this paper is not to derive the
full CKM matrix and not to claim a Clay result.  It records a narrower state:
the CKM `p=7` lane is now selected by independent CM and Hurwitz arguments;
the Klein quartic supplies a genuine three-generation object through
\(J(K) \simeq X_0(49)^3\); and the Navier-Stokes carrier bound has a precise
Kolmogorov/Serrin interpretation.  Three further results are documented only
as frontier items: Kolmogorov-calibrated viscous tail dominance with
\(K^*(\nu)=\frac34\log_2(1/\nu)\), the corrected \(PSL(2,\mathbb F_7)\)
texture constraint, and a finite \(\mathbb Z/7\) spectral gap.  The follow-on
frontier receipts sharpen these without promoting them: the algebraic
`T_r` tail energy is monotone and collapses after finite tail depth, but
actual NS-flow transfer is still blocked on an active-depth forward estimate;
the minimal \(V_3\) spurion route has one verified triple invariant in the
exterior determinant channel, so a rank-one heavy projector needs an extra
residual selector; and the finite cusp target is the scalar Hecke action on
\(S_2(X_0(49))\), not the reuse of a Cayley-graph gap.

The CKM claim boundary is deliberately conservative.  The Georgi-Jarlskog
relation for \(\lambda\) is treated as a literature compatibility, not as a
new DASHI theorem.  The estimate \(|V_{ub}| \approx \alpha_1\alpha_2\) is kept
only as a leading-order structural hypothesis in the \(U_d \approx I\)
approximation.  The quantities \(|V_{cb}|\), \(\gamma\), \(\beta\), \(\alpha\),
\(\rho\), and \(\eta\) are not carrier predictions at the present stage.  The
corrected \(PSL(2,\mathbb F_7)\) tensor constraint is likewise not a Yukawa
derivation: \(V_3\otimes V_3=V_3'\oplus V_6\), with no trivial representation,
so a symmetry-breaking input is still required before a down-type matrix
\(Y_d\) can be claimed.  A \(V_3\) spurion is the minimal recorded candidate
for that breaking, but the pure \(V_3\) channel is exterior/antisymmetric and
generically rank two.  The rank-one heavy-generation texture is an additional
residual-selector ansatz, not an inhabited Yukawa receipt.

The positive CKM architecture result is instead geometric.  The smooth
class-number-one CM point at `p=7`, the Hurwitz prime triple `(2,3,7)`, and
the Klein quartic all point to the same object.  The Jacobian of the Klein
quartic splits as \(X_0(49)^3\), and each factor carries CM by
\(\mathcal O_{-7}\).  This gives a real three-generation carrier object, while
leaving the labelling morphism and the down-type Yukawa dynamics open.
There is also a \(p=7\) Structural Convergence Remark: the six nonzero
directions in \(\mathbb F_7^\times\) pair into three sign-opposite direction
classes, giving the first odd-prime carrier with the directional capacity
expected of a three-slot architecture while still matching the Hurwitz
\((2,3,7)\) constraint.  This is recorded as consistency evidence for the lane
selection, not as a new theorem about mixing angles.  The same lane appears in
seven bounded characterisations: it sets the finite \(\mathbb Z/7\) product
spectral gap, it is the deepest generation-prime depth while still lying below
physical viscous cutoffs, it is the \(7^2=49\) level of the Klein-quartic
\(X_0(49)^3\) structure, it is where the full
\(PSL(2,\mathbb F_7)\) bilinear invariant no-go lives, it carries the CM
arithmetic reading, it admits the \(7\equiv1\pmod3\) arithmetic phase split,
and it matches the Hurwitz uniqueness lane.  No other prime has been found to
satisfy all seven characterisations, but independence of the seven
characterisations has not been proved.  It is therefore a structural
convergence remark, not a convergence theorem or phenomenological closure.

The p-adic carrier coordinate is used with the same discipline.  A real
comparison coordinate is decomposed as an integer projection plus a residual
defect,
\[
x=\lfloor x\rfloor+\{x\}.
\]
The integer part is the component that maps into the p-adic carrier through
\(\mathbb Z\to\mathbb Z_p\).  The decimal, or fractional, part is not treated
as a hidden p-adic datum; it remains a defect recorded in \(C\)-space.  In the
ternary lane the 3-adic attractor
\[
-\frac12 = 1+3+3^2+\cdots
\]
matches \(\operatorname{Re}(\tau_{-7})\) for
\(\tau_{-7}=(-1+i\sqrt7)/2\).  This match is an origin and coordinate
consistency remark, not a derivation of the physical CP phase.

This also clarifies the programme lineage.  One path is ternary and 3-adic:
carrier coordinates, fixed attractors, and the \(-1/2\) real projection.  The
other path is braid-theoretic: \(B_3\), Yang-Baxter consistency, and the
Hurwitz \((2,3,7)\) constraint.  In the present paper these paths are allowed
to converge only at the Klein quartic / \(p=7\) architecture.  They do not
confer independent authority on a CKM matrix.

The helical phase diagram chase is correspondingly partial-complete.  The
arithmetic splitting over \(\mathbb Z_7\) and the distinction of the three
characters \(\psi_k(2)=\omega^k\) are proved, using
\(\mathbb Z/3\mathbb Z=\{1,2,4\}\subset\mathbb F_7^\times\).  The branch
convention against the existing single-factor morphism \(\phi\) is corrected:
\(\phi\) matches the \(\psi_2\) branch because
\(\mathrm{Frob}_{p2}=4=2^2\).  This supersedes the stale zero-branch
commutativity wording.  It fixes branch bookkeeping only; it does not derive
physical generation labels, CKM entries, or \(Y_d\).

There is a new candidate selector to keep separate from the labelling theorem.
The depth grading
\[
\operatorname{depth}(p)=\lfloor \log_2(p)\rfloor
\]
assigns primes to coarse carrier depths and places \(p=7\) at depth \(2\).
This may be useful as a residual Yukawa-selector hypothesis, especially when
separating rank-one texture assumptions from the exterior \(V_3\) channel.
It is not a proof of a down-type Yukawa matrix, not a CKM entry calculation,
and not a replacement for the corrected \(\psi_2/\phi\) branch convention or
the remaining morphism and symmetry-breaking work.

The Navier-Stokes contribution is likewise bounded.  The carrier
\(H^{11/8}\) estimate is not promoted to a Clay theorem.  Instead the paper
records the exact dyadic computation connecting Kolmogorov
\(K^{-5/3}\) scaling to carrier geometric decay with
\(\alpha_K = 2^{-2/3}\), and it identifies the transfer problem as a
depth-control/norm-comparison question.  The viscous tail threshold
\(K^*(\nu)=\frac34\log_2(1/\nu)\) is recorded only under Kolmogorov
calibration.  Finite active depth gives standard Serrin regularity; unbounded
active depth only blocks this route, and does not imply blow-up.  The
MDL/tail bridge records the same point as a candidate theorem: the missing
mathematics is a forward estimate for the actual NS evolution.  That estimate
must be cumulative, not merely a pointwise band estimate: the tail above
\(K^*(\nu)\) has to be summed over active depths for the actual flow and then
compared to the archimedean Sobolev/Serrin norm.

FRACTRAN admissibility sharpens the carrier-side tail condition by imposing a
nonresonance screen on the algebraic tail model.  It is a refinement of the
tail-dominance heuristic, not a transfer theorem for actual Navier-Stokes
flow.

The adelic Sobolev comparison lane is also narrowed.  The checked p-adic
AdS/CFT and p-adic PDE literature does not supply the needed archimedean
domination theorem.  The live route is therefore an automorphic \(GL(1)\) /
Hecke-character comparison: one would need to show that the adelic character
norms controlling the carrier tail dominate the archimedean Sobolev quantity
used in the NS regularity argument.  This is a new proof obligation, not a
theorem imported from the existing literature.

The finite spectral-gap calculation has the same status discipline.  On the
finite \(\mathbb Z/7\) carrier, the unnormalised cycle gap is
\(2-2\cos(2\pi/7)\approx0.753\); the normalised random-walk gap is half this
value.  In the product carrier \(\mathbb Z/7\times\mathbb Z/3\times\mathbb Z/2\),
the unnormalised factor gaps are \(0.753\), \(3\), and \(2\), so the
\(\mathbb Z/7\) factor remains the bottleneck.  This is finite evidence only.
It is not a continuum mass gap, not a Yang-Mills theorem, and not a Clay
promotion.  The finite cusp/Hecke frontier is a separate computation on
\(S_2(X_0(49))\): the checked table is
\(a_2=1,a_3=0,a_5=0,a_7=0,a_{11}=4,a_{13}=a_{17}=a_{19}=0\), but the usual
cusp space is one-dimensional, so no intrinsic multi-eigenvalue Hecke gap is
computed.

This fail-closed convention is the paper's main discipline.  A formula is
promoted only when its mathematical origin, comparison target, and scope are
all specified.  Negative computations are retained next to positive receipts:
the direct angle \(\arctan(\sqrt 7)\), McKay-Thompson values, Weierstrass
stabiliser ordering, and a tested ultrametric Yukawa hierarchy have all been
retired.  The \(p=7\) convergence statement follows the same rule: the current
criterion checks that each of the seven readings is stated as an independent
input lane and that no checked prime other than \(7\) satisfies the whole
conjunction, but it does not prove full logical independence.  The result is
a paper about a precise carrier architecture and its remaining mathematical
boundaries, not a completed phenomenological model.
