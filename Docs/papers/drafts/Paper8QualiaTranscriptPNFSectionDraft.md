# Paper 8 Draft Note: Transcript PNF, Qualia Boundaries, and Observer Residuals

Date: `2026-06-14`
Status: concise section draft; non-promoting

## Scope

This note is a Paper 8 / publication-roadmap draft only. It proposes how to
describe PNF over qualitative transcripts without claiming access to
phenomenal content.

Core boundary:

```text
PNF over qualitative transcripts recovers report structure, not phenomenal
content.
```

The admissible claim is structural: transcript PNF can organize first-person,
third-person, legal, or experimental reports into roles, projections, residual
fibres, and missing-bridge obligations. It does not recover "what it is like",
private sensation, pain identity, animal consciousness, latent mental state, or
observer-invariant qualia.

## Paper 8 Placement

Paper 8 should treat transcript PNF as a controlled consumer of the closure
grammar:

```text
transcript / report
-> parser or role projection
-> PNF carrier
-> observer-manifold residual
-> no-promotion boundary
```

This belongs in Paper 8 because it illustrates projection-defect discipline.
The transcript's public structure can be quotiented, compared, and residualized.
The missing phenomenal bridge remains visible as a residual rather than being
silently promoted to content recovery.

Suggested section title:

```text
Transcript PNF and the hard boundary of qualitative reports
```

## Observer-Manifold Residual

The right Paper 8 wording is:

```text
An observer manifold records the coordinates under which a report is projected:
speaker, modality, time, context, role vocabulary, admissibility gate, and
available comparison law. The residual is the difference between the public
report structure recovered by PNF and the stronger content claim a reader might
be tempted to infer.
```

For qualitative transcripts, the dominant residual is phenomenal:

```text
public report structure != private phenomenal content
```

The residual should remain named in the manuscript. It is not an error term to
be optimized away unless a future bridge supplies independent authority for the
stronger claim.

## Candidate Manuscript Paragraph

PNF may be applied to qualitative transcripts, including animal-observation
reports, Nagel-style bat examples, altered-state or Leary-style introspective
reports, and SensibLaw affidavit-like materials. In each case the recovered
object is the public structure of the report: role assignments, temporal
ordering, projection context, comparison hooks, and residual fibres. The method
does not recover phenomenal content. The observer-manifold residual is exactly
the unpromoted gap between structured report and private experience, and Paper
8 records that gap as a claim boundary rather than a solved bridge.
