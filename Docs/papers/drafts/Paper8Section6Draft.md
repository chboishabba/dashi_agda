# Section 6. Open Problems

The previous sections isolate a finite arithmetic framework and a set of
candidate gauge labels.  The remaining problems are not editorial caveats; they
are the mathematical and physical gates that prevent promotion to a Standard
Model, Yang-Mills, Navier-Stokes, or full-unification theorem.

## 6.1 CKM Completion

The \(V_{us}\) and \(V_{cb}\) lanes have strong diagnostic structure, but the
CKM matrix is not derived.  The main open entries are \(V_{ub}\), the physical
CP phase, and the normalization that turns the candidate complex Yukawa entry
into a physical diagonalisation problem.

Current \(V_{ub}\) routes are diagnostic only.  The degree-28/isogeny and
carrier-RG variants improve some numerical surfaces but still require external
QCD, physical Yukawa normalization, and PDG authority binding.  The CP phase is
also open: the positive real CM period convention gives a diagnostic-negative
phase, while the Hecke \(p=29\) phase is only a candidate source.  A valid
solution must specify the embedding, phase convention, diagonalisation
authority, and comparison target in one coherent receipt.

## 6.2 \(SU(2)\)-\(SU(3)\) Decoupling

The gauge section treats \(SU(2)_3\) and the color \(SU(3)\) candidate as
sectorally distinct.  The intended mechanism is the lepton-quark split via
SET inequivalence: weak-isospin and color data live in inequivalent enriched
sectors, so level-rank adjacency does not collapse them into a single mixed
gauge factor.

This remains a pending proof.  The required result should formalize the
sector labels, the admissible intertwiners, and the obstruction to an
unwanted \(SU(2)\)-\(SU(3)\) identification.  Until that result is verified,
the direct-product Standard Model gauge reading remains candidate-labelled.

## 6.3 Dimensionful Scale And VEV

The carrier gives dimensionless ratios and arithmetic normalisations more
naturally than dimensionful masses.  The electroweak VEV, Higgs mass scale,
and any dimensionful Yang-Mills or fermion mass scale currently require an
external anchor.  Existing Higgs and Yukawa comparisons should therefore be
read as calibrated diagnostics, not as derivations of the physical VEV.

A closed account would need an internal scale-setting theorem, or an explicit
authority boundary that explains exactly which physical datum is imported and
which predictions remain independent after that import.

## 6.4 Navier-Stokes Regularity

The Navier-Stokes lane has finite weak-solution and compactness-facing receipt
surfaces, but it does not prove global smooth regularity.  The open mechanism
is vorticity control.  In particular, the programme still lacks a uniform
large-data estimate that prevents vortex stretching from producing a continuum
singularity and that survives the carrier-to-continuum passage.

The current state is therefore compatible with a roadmap through weak
solutions, Aubin-Lions compactness, elliptic bootstrap, and BKM-style criteria,
but no global regularity theorem or Clay promotion follows.

## 6.5 Yang-Mills Continuum And Tightness

The Yang-Mills lane has finite-lattice and strong-coupling diagnostics, but
the continuum constructive theory is open.  The missing analytic step is a
tightness and continuum-limit proof for the Wilson/gauge-field measures along
the required scaling trajectory, together with the preservation of reflection
positivity, gauge covariance, nontriviality, and a uniform positive mass gap.

Finite gap diagnostics and carrier OS surfaces do not replace this proof.  A
valid continuum result must construct the limiting measure or Wightman theory
with the required axioms and then show that the mass gap survives the limit.
Until then, the Yang-Mills material remains a finite-depth and conditional
blocker map, not a Clay mass-gap proof.

## 6.6 Summary Of Non-Promotions

| Topic | Present status | Promotion blocker |
| --- | --- | --- |
| \(V_{ub}\) | Diagnostic routes only | Physical Yukawa normalization, QCD authority, CKM diagonalisation |
| CP phase | Candidate Hecke phase; current CM-period route negative | Non-real phase source and convention binding |
| \(SU(2)\)-\(SU(3)\) split | Candidate SET-inequivalence mechanism | Formal decoupling proof pending |
| VEV and mass scale | External calibration | Internal scale-setting theorem absent |
| Navier-Stokes | Weak/compactness roadmap | No global vorticity-control mechanism |
| Yang-Mills | Finite/conditional lattice and OS surfaces | No continuum tightness and Wightman/mass-gap proof |

These are open problems in the mathematical sense.  They are not assumed away
by the candidate gauge labels or by numerical agreement in lower-dimensional
diagnostics.
