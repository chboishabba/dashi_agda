## Executive Finding

The reader meets claim-control language in the title block, core formalism, abstract, introduction, related work, origin story, each construction lane, empirical section, compression section, cross-domain section, obstruction table, reproducibility section, non-claim section, and conclusion.

Recommended edit strategy: keep one compact closure-discipline subsection near the end of the core architecture setup, keep one lane-local boundary sentence where each lane closes, and move repeated long forbidden-claim inventories into the obstruction frontier plus the non-claims section.

## Proposed Compact Subsection

Recommended location: replace or expand Section 5.4 (`Closure Semantics as Enforcement`, lines 373-385). After this subsection exists, later sections can use brief lane-local closure sentences instead of repeated full disclaimers.

````markdown
### 5.4 Closure Discipline

Closure in DASHI is a mathematical typing rule, not an editorial disclaimer. A
surface promotes only the projection whose evidence is inhabited at that
surface. The residual part remains visible as a defect: a missing quotient law,
adapter, authority token, convention receipt, calibration, or upstream
constructor.

This gives the manuscript one discipline for all lanes. A theorem surface may
promote a local algebraic statement; an empirical receipt may promote a bounded
comparison; a boundary record may promote only the fact that a stronger bridge
is still absent. Diagnostics, analogies, provenance, and request surfaces can
motivate later work, but they do not promote physics-facing claims by prose.

The positive claim form is therefore:

```text
surface S promotes projection P_s(x);
residual D_s(x) records the first missing bridge for stronger claim Q.
```

Section 10 collects the current residual frontier. The intervening sections use
this rule locally: each lane states its promoted surface, its first residual
obligation, and no stronger reading unless the corresponding surface is
inhabited.
````

## Suggested Local Rewrite Pattern

Use this pattern at the end of each lane:

```text
The promoted result is [specific positive surface]. The first residual

therefore deferred to the closure frontier in Section 10.
```

This keeps the derivation moving while preserving fail-closed rigor.
