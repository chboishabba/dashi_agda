# Empirical Contact: Drell-Yan Receipts and Authority Frontier

Status: Lane 3 manuscript draft; paper-facing, non-promoting beyond the typed
surfaces named here.

## Scope

The empirical-contact lane in Paper 1 is intentionally narrow. It is not a
closure claim for the DASHI unification program, and it is not an accepted
external empirical authority. Its role is to show that the constructive
unification architecture can bind a named physics observable to a frozen,
mechanically tracked receipt while still blocking stronger claims whose
authority, convention, or strict-fit obligations are missing.

The current positive surface is the bounded W3 below-Z Drell-Yan
comparison-law receipt over CMS-SMP-20-003 / HEPData `ins2079374/t43`, using
the `ins2079374/t44` covariance convention. This surface is recorded by
`DASHI.Physics.Closure.HEPDataW3ComparisonLawReceipt.canonicalHEPDataW3ComparisonLawReceipt`
and is attached to the FactorVec falsification-lane protocol by
`DASHI.Physics.Closure.DrellYanAdjacentRatioEmpiricalLaneReceipt.canonicalDrellYanAdjacentRatioEmpiricalLaneReceipt`.

The paper should describe this as bounded empirical contact, not as completed
empirical closure. The receipt is local to the `t43` below-Z adjacent-ratio
surface and explicitly does not promote the above-Z `t45/t46` lane, W4/W5
convention closure, accepted W3 authority, physical calibration, stress-tensor,
or full empirical adequacy.

## Strict Protocols Fail

Two stricter protocols are explicitly negative and must remain visible in the
manuscript.

First, the adjacent-ratio receipt records that the stricter
`chi2/dof <= 2` falsification-lane target is not satisfied:

```text

```

This does not erase the bounded W3 receipt. It separates a local
comparison-law pass from a stronger strict empirical criterion.

Second, the strict log-covariance diagnostic is a separate stronger protocol
recorded by
`DASHI.Physics.Closure.DrellYanStrictLogLinearSubspaceReceipt.canonicalDrellYanStrictLogLinearSubspaceReceipt`.
It is also negative:

| `sigmaDashiV4Predictor` | `3180.211733150705` | false | Failure dominated by `span(1, log(phiStar))` |

The strict-log receipt is still scientifically useful because it localizes the
failure. The current diagnostic records that full covariance reduces chi-square
relative to diagonal-only covariance; the failure is therefore not explained by
covariance inflation. The main obstruction is the log-linear residual
subspace. The next theorem target is not prose promotion, but a frozen no-refit
route discharging `StrictPassOrthogonalityObligation`: derive or accept the
log-linear shape law, prove orthogonality to `span(1, log(phiStar))`, match the
intercept, and bound the orthogonal residual complement under a
depth-averaged curvature kernel.

## W3 Authority Boundary

The bounded W3 comparison-law receipt is not the same object as accepted W3
empirical authority. The accepted-authority type remains constructorless at the
canonical boundary: no accepted provider token, non-postulated policy hook, or
governance payload currently inhabits `W3AcceptedEvidenceAuthorityToken`.

Paper 1 may therefore say that a bounded W3 empirical-contact receipt exists.
It must not say that W3 has been externally accepted, that the Drell-Yan lane
confirms DASHI, or that the empirical anchor for full unification is closed.

The first missing W3 object is an accepted authority payload binding the
provider identity, CMS/HEPData sources, frozen commit, covariance/source
convention, comparison law, and non-collapse witness at the canonical token
boundary.

## W4/W5 Convention Boundary

W4 and W5 remain blocked by convention and authority, not by a sentence that
can be repaired editorially. The accepted Drell-Yan luminosity/convention
authority needed for W4/W5 is still missing. Local numeric probes, CT18-style
experiments, packet drafts, or self-populated convention records are
non-promoting unless they are accepted at the canonical boundary.

For W4, the missing convention authority blocks any claim of Z-peak adequacy,
Candidate256 physical calibration, matter-field construction, stress-tensor
construction, sourced GR, or calibrated physical-unit result.

For W5, the available W3 `t43` adjacent-ratio receipt does not supply the
observable needed for a positive W5 correction claim. W5 needs an accepted
`R45/R43`-style correction result under a shared Drell-Yan convention,
including the accepted luminosity pair, PDF set and version, LHAPDF member,
checksums, scale convention, rapidity and mass-bin rules, flavour weights,
integration method, source citation, tolerance, and no-manual-overfit
attestation.
