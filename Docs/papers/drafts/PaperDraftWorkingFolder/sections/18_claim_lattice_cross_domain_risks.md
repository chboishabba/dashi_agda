# 18. Claim-Lattice Cross-Domain Risk Audit

Status: `docs-only; claim-risk audit; non-promoting; living document`

Maintenance rule: update this section whenever a new domain claim is proposed
for Paper 1 or for a follow-on paper. The purpose is to keep each new
cross-domain, empirical, biological, perceptual, topological, or outreach
claim in the correct lattice position before manuscript prose can promote it.

## Formal Model

O: This section audits the paper-facing claim lattice around constructive TOE
framing, the cross-domain variational spine, and outreach-derived explanatory
language.

R: Identify tempting overclaims introduced by combining constructive TOE
language, the typed cross-domain variational spine, and outreach/future-work
documents; replace each with admissible wording that preserves the current
fail-closed boundary.

C: This audit is constrained by
`Docs/papers/drafts/PaperDraftWorkingFolder/Paper1_Manuscript.md`,
`Docs/papers/drafts/PaperDraftWorkingFolder/ClaimLedger.md`, `README.md`,
`Docs/CurrentGateStatus.md`, and `Docs/MathProfOutreachSummary.md`. It writes
only this owned section and does not edit theorem files or claim ledgers.

S: The current paper can positively claim a fail-closed constructive closure
architecture, selected bounded formal surfaces, and the existence of a
structural cross-domain projection-defect / MDL object. It cannot promote that
object into quantitative physics, chemistry, biology, perception, GR, or TOE
closure.

L: The relevant lattice has three levels: admissible structural object,
request/future-work theorem targets, and forbidden promoted domain claims. A
sentence may move upward only through an inhabited receipt, theorem, authority
token, or canonical audit surface.

P: Use the table below as an editorial rewrite guide and as a proposed update
source for `ClaimLedger.md`.

G: Preserve fail-closed posture. Structural bridges, diagnostics, outreach
summaries, request packs, and theorem-thin local examples do not become
empirical authority, physical calibration, or full unification.

F: The main residual risk is rhetorical compression: phrases such as
"same object", "TOE", "stable scientific object", "protein funnel", "Kluver
orbit", "contraction implies quadratic", or "time-symmetric histories" can
sound like completed domain recovery unless each is pinned to the current
typed boundary.

## Bottom Line

The cross-domain variational spine is useful because it gives the paper a
single typed vocabulary for otherwise distant theorem targets. Its admissible
claim is schema-level: a shared projection-defect / MDL shape exists as a
structural bridge. The paper must not present that bridge as recovered
chemistry, biology, perception, physical calibration, or TOE closure.
