## Boundary Rule

The braid-memory material belongs outside the promoted Paper 1 claim set. It
may be used as an outreach-facing example of why hidden topological state,
path-dependent transport, braid-group actions, and observation quotients are
interesting mathematical targets. It may not be used as empirical support for
DASHI.

The boundary has two separate statements. First, the external Nature/Phys.org
material is evidence about a specific even-denominator fractional quantum Hall
experimental system and about the physical realizability of anyon
interferometry targets. Second, DASHI's braid-memory vocabulary is a theoretical
future bridge shape. No current DASHI receipt identifies that experimental
system as an instance of DASHI's construction, and the paper must not collapse
these two statements into one claim.

The safe manuscript role is:

> Recent anyon-interferometry work provides external evidence that the relevant
> physical target class is experimentally serious, and it motivates future
> theorem shapes involving hidden topological state, braid actions, and quotient
> observations. DASHI does not currently claim to predict, explain, or
> instantiate those experiments; it only has a governance framework for stating
> what a later typed bridge would have to prove before any such connection
> became paper-admissible.

This is parallel to the GR and Drell-Yan boundaries. A physically suggestive
experiment can help name an obligation, but the obligation is not satisfied
until the owning DASHI lane supplies the exact receipt and the manuscript cites
that receipt as the authority.

## Theorem-Shape Lemma

The following is a future theorem shape, not a present theorem.

```text
Lemma shape: Braid-memory observation quotient

Given:
  H      a hidden topological state space or sheet carrier;
  B_n    a braid group acting on H by a typed action rho : B_n -> Aut(H);
  Q      an observation carrier;
  pi     an observation quotient pi : H -> Q;
  gamma1 gamma2 : B_n two braid words.

Future obligation:
  construct conditions under which

    pi (rho gamma1 h) = pi (rho gamma2 h)

  captures observational indistinguishability, while

    rho gamma1 h != rho gamma2 h

  remains a hidden-sheet distinction whenever the braid words differ by a
  nontrivial topological sector not collapsed by the intended quotient.

Required receipts:
  1. a concrete hidden-sheet carrier H;
  2. a well-typed braid-group action rho;
  3. an observation quotient pi with kernel/equivalence laws;
  4. a nontriviality witness separating hidden state from observed quotient;
  5. a no-promotion audit stating that no empirical experiment is claimed
     unless an accepted physics bridge and authority token are inhabited.
```

The useful analogy is formal: order-sensitive transport can live in a hidden
state while measurement exposes only a quotient. That analogy is not a claim
that DASHI has identified the physical hidden state, the relevant anyons, the
Hamiltonian, the interferometer model, or the experimental observable.
