# 1. Introduction

DASHI is a fail-closed arithmetic programme for recording which pieces of a
carrier-based physics construction are proved, which are diagnostic, and which
have been explicitly retired.  The purpose of this paper is not to derive the
full CKM matrix and not to claim a Clay result.  It records a narrower state:
the CKM `p=7` lane is now selected by independent CM and Hurwitz arguments;
the Klein quartic supplies a genuine three-generation object through
\(J(K) \simeq X_0(49)^3\); and the Navier-Stokes carrier bound has a precise
Kolmogorov/Serrin interpretation.  Three further results are documented only
as frontier items: Kolmogorov-calibrated viscous tail dominance with
\(K^*(\nu)=\frac34\log_2(1/\nu)\), the corrected \(PSL(2,\mathbb F_7)\)
texture constraint, and a finite \(\mathbb Z/7\) spectral gap.

The CKM claim boundary is deliberately conservative.  The Georgi-Jarlskog
relation for \(\lambda\) is treated as a literature compatibility, not as a
new DASHI theorem.  The estimate \(|V_{ub}| \approx \alpha_1\alpha_2\) is kept
only as a leading-order structural hypothesis in the \(U_d \approx I\)
approximation.  The quantities \(|V_{cb}|\), \(\gamma\), \(\beta\), \(\alpha\),
\(\rho\), and \(\eta\) are not carrier predictions at the present stage.  The
corrected \(PSL(2,\mathbb F_7)\) tensor constraint is likewise not a Yukawa
derivation: \(V_3\otimes V_3=V_3'\oplus V_6\), with no trivial representation,
so a symmetry-breaking input is still required before a down-type matrix
\(Y_d\) can be claimed.

The positive CKM architecture result is instead geometric.  The smooth
class-number-one CM point at `p=7`, the Hurwitz prime triple `(2,3,7)`, and
the Klein quartic all point to the same object.  The Jacobian of the Klein
quartic splits as \(X_0(49)^3\), and each factor carries CM by
\(\mathcal O_{-7}\).  This gives a real three-generation carrier object, while
leaving the labelling morphism and the down-type Yukawa dynamics open.
There is also a structural bottleneck at \(p=7\): the six nonzero directions in
\(\mathbb F_7^\times\) pair into three sign-opposite direction classes, giving
the first odd-prime carrier with the directional capacity expected of a
three-slot architecture while still matching the Hurwitz \((2,3,7)\)
constraint.  This is recorded as consistency evidence for the lane selection,
not as a new theorem about mixing angles.
The same lane appears as the bottleneck in several bounded senses: it sets the
finite \(\mathbb Z/7\) product spectral gap, it is the deepest generation-prime
depth while still lying below physical viscous cutoffs, it is the \(7^2=49\)
level of the Klein-quartic \(X_0(49)^3\) structure, and it is where the full
\(PSL(2,\mathbb F_7)\) bilinear invariant no-go lives.  These coincidences are
kept as a structural consistency remark, not as phenomenological closure.

The p-adic carrier coordinate is used with the same discipline.  A real
comparison coordinate is decomposed as an integer projection plus a residual
defect,
\[
x=\lfloor x\rfloor+\{x\}.
\]
The integer part is the component that maps into the p-adic carrier through
\(\mathbb Z\to\mathbb Z_p\).  The decimal, or fractional, part is not treated
as a hidden p-adic datum; it remains a defect recorded in \(C\)-space.  In the
ternary lane the 3-adic attractor
\[
-\frac12 = 1+3+3^2+\cdots
\]
matches \(\operatorname{Re}(\tau_{-7})\) for
\(\tau_{-7}=(-1+i\sqrt7)/2\).  This match is an origin and coordinate
consistency remark, not a derivation of the physical CP phase.

This also clarifies the programme lineage.  One path is ternary and 3-adic:
carrier coordinates, fixed attractors, and the \(-1/2\) real projection.  The
other path is braid-theoretic: \(B_3\), Yang-Baxter consistency, and the
Hurwitz \((2,3,7)\) constraint.  In the present paper these paths are allowed
to converge only at the Klein quartic / \(p=7\) architecture.  They do not
confer independent authority on a CKM matrix.

The Navier-Stokes contribution is likewise bounded.  The carrier
\(H^{11/8}\) estimate is not promoted to a Clay theorem.  Instead the paper
records the exact dyadic computation connecting Kolmogorov
\(K^{-5/3}\) scaling to carrier geometric decay with
\(\alpha_K = 2^{-2/3}\), and it identifies the transfer problem as a
depth-control/norm-comparison question.  The viscous tail threshold
\(K^*(\nu)=\frac34\log_2(1/\nu)\) is recorded only under Kolmogorov
calibration.  Finite active depth gives standard Serrin regularity; unbounded
active depth only blocks this route, and does not imply blow-up.

The finite spectral-gap calculation has the same status discipline.  On the
finite \(\mathbb Z/7\) carrier, the unnormalised cycle gap is
\(2-2\cos(2\pi/7)\approx0.753\); the normalised random-walk gap is half this
value.  In the product carrier \(\mathbb Z/7\times\mathbb Z/3\times\mathbb Z/2\),
the unnormalised factor gaps are \(0.753\), \(3\), and \(2\), so the
\(\mathbb Z/7\) factor remains the bottleneck.  This is finite evidence only.
It is not a
continuum mass gap, not a Yang-Mills theorem, and not a Clay promotion.

This fail-closed convention is the paper's main discipline.  A formula is
promoted only when its mathematical origin, comparison target, and scope are
all specified.  Negative computations are retained next to positive receipts:
the direct angle \(\arctan(\sqrt 7)\), McKay-Thompson values, Weierstrass
stabiliser ordering, and a tested ultrametric Yukawa hierarchy have all been
retired.  The result is a paper about a precise carrier architecture and its
remaining mathematical boundaries, not a completed phenomenological model.
