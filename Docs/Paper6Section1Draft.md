# 1. Introduction

DASHI is a fail-closed arithmetic programme for extracting Standard Model
diagnostics from the CM geometry of imaginary quadratic fields.  The purpose
of this paper is deliberately narrower than a derivation of the full CKM
matrix.  We isolate two Wolfenstein-sector quantities whose carrier formulas
are now sharp enough to state as sub-percent arithmetic diagnostics, and we
record the remaining CKM entries as open problems rather than promoting them
by numerical proximity.

The first result is the Cabibbo parameter.  With
\(\alpha_1 = \sqrt{m_u/m_c}\), the discriminant \(-3\) CM lane gives
\[
\lambda = \alpha_1 \pi\sqrt{3}.
\]
Using the current carrier convention this gives \(\lambda = 0.22441\), to be
compared with the reference value \(0.22500\), an error of \(-0.26\%\).  The
second result is the \(|V_{cb}|\) lane.  The inter-generation degree is
identified with the degree of the inert Hecke correspondence \(T_{13}\) on the
Hilbert modular surface for \(\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{21})\), namely
\(\deg(T_{13}) = 14\).  With modular-volume normalisation this gives
\(|V_{cb}| = 0.04072\), to be compared with \(0.04080\), an error of
\(-0.20\%\).

The paper's claim boundary is part of the result.  The quantities above are
not fitted parameters in a texture model: the constants are fixed by CM
volume, Hecke degree, and the recorded lane normalisation.  At the same time,
the construction does not derive \(|V_{ub}|\), the CP-violating phase, the
Jarlskog invariant, or the full physical CKM matrix.  Existing candidate
routes for \(|V_{ub}|\) miss badly or depend on an unidentified CP mechanism,
and are therefore reported as diagnostics only.

This fail-closed convention is essential.  A formula is promoted only when its
mathematical origin, numerical convention, and comparison target are all
specified.  Negative and incomplete results are retained in the same receipt
tower as successful ones.  The result is a paper about two arithmetic
Wolfenstein diagnostics and their surrounding obstruction map, not a claim
that DASHI has completed quark mixing.
