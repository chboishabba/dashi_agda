# Phase 2 Programme

Status: coordination programme; non-promoting.

This document records the next proof-programme phase after the roadmap
execution tranche. It is documentation only. It does not promote Clay
Navier-Stokes, Clay Yang-Mills, Wightman reconstruction, Standard Model
reconstruction, or terminal/unification claims.

## Boundary

Phase 2 is a blocker-reduction phase, not a prize-claim phase.

- Weak Navier-Stokes is not Clay Navier-Stokes.
- A carrier weak-form or Leray-style branch does not imply smooth global
  regularity.
- Finite-lattice or strong-coupling Yang-Mills is not continuum Clay
  Yang-Mills.
- A carrier-unit mass-gap estimate is not a physical continuum mass gap.
- No phase-2 item may remove a promotion boundary unless the required
  continuum theorem and external authority boundary are both discharged.

## Navier-Stokes Lane

Phase 2 treats the weak NS branch as complete at receipt scope through the
prime-band Littlewood-Paley route: prime-band projectors, Bernstein estimates,
Bony paraproduct decomposition, Leray energy, Aubin-Lions compactness,
nonlinear passage, weak formulation, and the weak-solution summary are all
recorded. That completion is not a Clay result.

The open NS branch is regularity:

| Target | Phase 2 task | Non-promotion boundary |
|---|---|---|
| NS-R1 | Use the prime-band Littlewood-Paley/Besov/parabolic-smoothing interface as the active replacement for the closed Haar-frame route. | Weak existence only; no smoothness or uniqueness follows. |
| NS-R2 | Prove the critical Besov estimate needed for regularity. | No Clay promotion follows without the global regularity estimates. |
| NS-R3 | Connect any carrier estimate to Archimedean vorticity control `||omega||_infty`. | This is the BKM-critical blocker, not a completed lemma. |
| NS-R4 | Bind external authority for local smooth existence and BKM continuation hypotheses. | Authority binding does not prove the missing global estimate. |

Phase 2 NS success means a critical-Besov or vorticity-control advance. It
does not mean Clay NS success unless global enstrophy/vorticity control, BKM
integrability, and smooth continuation are proved.

## Yang-Mills Lane

Phase 2 treats YM L1 as finite-lattice inhabited and YM L2 as partially
inhabited only in finite strong coupling. The corrected coupling status
rejects `beta = alpha1` as the Wilson beta, records the CS-level candidate
`alpha_s = 1/3`, `beta_SU2 = 3/pi`, finite string tension `2.125`, and a
`2.33 GeV` finite-lattice mass-gap diagnostic.

The open YM branch is the continuum interface:

| Target | Phase 2 task | Non-promotion boundary |
|---|---|---|
| YM-C1 | Separate depth-uniform finite strong-coupling constants from cutoff-removal claims. | Uniform-in-depth carrier estimates are not continuum tightness. |
| YM-C2 | Study the candidate `k -> infinity` CS-level family as the Wilson continuum trajectory. | The family is named, not proved tight. |
| YM-C3 | Identify the exact tightness topology and correlator bounds needed for L3/L4. | No continuum measure is constructed by finite estimates alone. |
| YM-C4 | Keep the finite mass-gap estimate separate from physical Wightman mass gap. | Carrier units do not supply a dimensionful continuum spectrum. |

Phase 2 YM success means a sharper L2-to-L3 blocker map. It does not mean
Clay YM success unless continuum construction, OS/Wightman reconstruction, and
gap survival are proved.

## Deliverables

1. A revised NS regularity blocker note centered on Littlewood-Paley/Besov
   estimates and BKM-critical vorticity control.
2. A revised YM L2-to-L3 note separating finite strong-coupling estimates from
   continuum beta running and tightness.
3. A promotion-boundary audit confirming that weak NS and finite/strong-coupling
   YM remain non-Clay.

## Stop Conditions

Stop and mark the branch open if a task requires any of the following without a
proved receipt or accepted external authority:

- continuum `L2(R3)` compactness from carrier coefficients;
- global `||omega||_infty` or BKM integrability control;
- beta-to-infinity Yang-Mills continuum trajectory;
- continuum OS axioms for gauge Yang-Mills;
- Wightman reconstruction;
- continuum spectral-gap survival.
