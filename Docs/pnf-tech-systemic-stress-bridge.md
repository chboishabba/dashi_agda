# PNF → tech/systemic-stress bridge

This bridge connects the repository's existing PNF spectral-field and braid-transport architecture to the technology/systemic-stress and quotient-MDL crisis stack.

It does **not** turn PNF similarity, news alignment, or evidence braids into a market prediction. Its job is narrower: preserve provenance, temporal revisions, contradictions, support changes, residual terminations, and candidate-reference paths around a detected BAD window.

## Reused PNF architecture

The bridge imports:

- `DASHI.Interop.PNFBraidTransportField`
- `DASHI.Interop.PNFSpectralFieldArchitectureReceipt`
- `DASHI.Promotion.PNFSpectralEmpiricalBasinValidation`

The PNF architecture already separates raw evidence, evidence-span PNF emission, corrected product-base summaries, residual graph/Laplacian structure, graph/DAG/tree projections, braid transport, spectral coordinates, vector-index lookup, resolver/selector processing, ITIR validation, and candidate output.

## Cross-pollination

PNF can contribute:

- source-event binding for a BAD window;
- revision and correction history;
- contradiction and support-strengthening/weakening traces;
- evidence-fibre residual termination;
- historical analogue candidates;
- receipted scenario-attribution candidates.

The crisis stack separately contributes market observations, phase classification, quotient/compression-fracture diagnostics, the funding/transmission chain, and MDL/holdout validation.

The combined model therefore keeps four questions separate:

1. What evidence and revisions surround the BAD window?
2. Where does stress appear concentrated?
3. Is the selected quotient/model ceasing to compress observations?
4. Is the financial transmission mechanism independently observed and validated?

## Two residual meanings

A PNF residual concerns typed meet/join, contradiction, support mismatch, or transport termination between evidence fibres. An economic residual concerns model mismatch and loss of compression in market observations.

They may corroborate one another, but the bridge records `corroboratedButDistinctResiduals` rather than identifying them.

## Hard gates

A PNF scenario-evidence receipt requires source binding, revision lineage, braid-signature binding, resolver/selector/ITIR receipts, event/market clock alignment, no lookahead, complete source-side-information accounting, and explicit non-promotion boundaries for transport, vectors, historical analogues, and news alignment.

The stronger joined gate additionally requires the existing validated tech/crisis quotient-MDL gate, including independent market-plumbing observations and transmission evidence.

## Canonical fixtures

The canonical PNF architecture is intentionally not commit-ready: resolver, selector, ITIR, clock-alignment, and complete side-information receipts remain absent. Therefore the canonical AI-capex and Greece-style fixtures remain `unsupportedLevel` as systemic-mechanism claims.

A fully receipted PNF attachment may accompany an independently validated crisis receipt, but it does not upgrade the mechanism promotion level. Promotion remains projected from the crisis/transmission/MDL receipt.

## Runtime deliverables

1. Emit a stable BAD-window identifier and exact market interval.
2. Bind each source event to publication, ingestion, and revision times plus URL/content digests.
3. Emit PNF source, fibre, crossing, residual, and braid-signature receipts.
4. Run resolver and selector without forward outcomes.
5. Preserve rejected candidates and search multiplicity as side information.
6. Align PNF event clocks with market windows explicitly.
7. Compare historical analogue ranking on train/holdout splits.
8. Build the market-plumbing and transmission receipt independently.
9. Report attribution, compression regime, mechanism status, and MDL status separately.
10. Never convert an adverse trit or PNF candidate into an automatic short or production trade.
