# Braided emotion process boundary

## Status

Candidate-only formal carrier and repository integration facade. This surface is not a clinical theory, diagnostic ontology, therapeutic protocol, or claim that one disputed emotion theory has won.

## Core claim

An emotion episode is represented as a temporally extended mode of organism-world coordination. The formal carrier keeps the following strands coupled but non-identical:

- bodily regulation;
- interoception;
- predictive interpretation;
- appraisal and significance;
- action readiness;
- concept and language;
- social and relational situation;
- conscious experience.

The compact statement is:

> Emotion is a constrained construction enacted by embodied organisms in consequential relationships; labels and theories remain useful, revisable, scale-indexed, and non-authoritative.

## Why this is a braid rather than a fusion

The implementation does not average competing theories into a single vague account. It records each theory family with a strongest question, a preserved insight, and a known blind spot. Local adequacy is allowed; global reduction is not required.

`BraidedEmotionTheoryContention` strengthens this by indexing theories against typed explanatory scales and disputed commitments. It preserves incompatible positions concerning natural kinds, biological universality, conceptual necessity, language constitution, neural specificity, world involvement, and relational constitution. No global winner is declared.

The knowledge braid keeps provenance, method, warrant, purpose, and obligation explicit across neural-experimental, phenomenological, relational, cultural-narrative, and ethical routes. These routes may constrain one another, but none is marked replaceable by another route.

The repository integration facade imports `RelationalQiBodyMemoryBridge` directly. Its existing boundaries against extraction, substitution for living practice, romantic authority, universalisation, and empirical capture are therefore inherited rather than recreated as a parallel vocabulary.

## Observation separation

An emotion label is not identified with the episode. The observation bridge distinguishes:

- the temporally extended episode;
- bodily residual proxies;
- neural measurement proxies;
- first-person situated report;
- public emotion labels;
- relational context.

None of the proxy or report surfaces is granted diagnostic authority or causal closure. A label remains provisional and revisable, with all four possibilities retained: it may disclose, compress, reshape, or obscure the episode.

The practical boundary is neither label-first nor label-free. The canonical observation sequence is:

1. low-inference sensation;
2. action tendency;
3. appraisal and context;
4. candidate label;
5. relational obligation;
6. chosen action.

Action may proceed without perfect classification, and no clinical protocol is claimed.
