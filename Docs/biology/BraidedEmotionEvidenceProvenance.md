# Braided emotion evidence provenance

`DASHI.Biology.BraidedEmotionEvidenceProvenance` binds evidence rows to stable
citation keys, population/task descriptions, years, and bounded claim scopes.

The initial keys are:

- `Barrett2017TheoryConstructedEmotion`
- `ElfenbeinAmbady2002EmotionRecognitionMetaAnalysis`
- `Gendron2015CultureEmotionPerception`
- `MenonUddin2010SalienceNetwork`
- `TugadeFredrickson2004EmotionalGranularity`
- `Kimmerer2013BraidingSweetgrass`

The formal relation between a source and a claim is one of:

- `supports`
- `qualifies`
- `conflictsWith`
- `underdetermines`

This lets one source support a bounded claim while another qualifies or conflicts
with a stronger version. Cross-cultural recognition evidence can therefore
support recurrent affective structure while cultural and task variation
qualifies context-independent natural-kind claims.

The Kimmerer receipt is normative and provenance-bearing, not empirical
neuroscience. It records material inspiration for reciprocity and braided
knowledge while preserving the no-formalisation, no-permission, no-substitution,
and anti-extraction boundaries. “Dialectical braid” remains DASHI terminology.

No receipt grants theorem, clinical, diagnostic, treatment, or cultural
authority. Full bibliographies and source-specific limitations remain in
`Docs/biology/BraidedEmotionEvidenceSources.md` and
`Docs/acknowledgements/RobinWallKimmerer.md`.
