# Philosophy Closure Laws and Instances

This tranche advances the merged philosophy architecture from governed carrier surfaces to executable laws, constructive promotion conditions, provenance adapters, and evidence-gated worked examples.

## Executable laws

`DASHI.Philosophy.LocalChartLaws` proves chart round trips, `T_ii = id`, `T_jk o T_ij = T_ik`, and recovery of the marked point from the local unit.

`DASHI.Philosophy.PartialChartCategory` packages situated charts as objects and admissible partial translations as morphisms. Identity and associative composition are required; inverses are available only when separately witnessed. Failed translations stay outside the morphism type.

`DASHI.Philosophy.AtlasCompatibilityLaws` adds overlap domains, asymmetric costs, residuals, failed-translation receipts, and voice preservation.

`DASHI.Philosophy.ResidualCompositionOrder` supplies the ordered-monoid interface needed for bounds of the form `R_AC <= R_AB + R_BC`, including monotonicity and iterated composition.

`DASHI.Philosophy.ProcessCompositionLaws` gives ordered path composition and associativity. `ProcessHistoryEquivalence` distinguishes literal process identity, process equivalence, and endpoint equality.

## Constructive leverage and topology

`MaterialLeverageClosure` packages reflexivity, atlas, organisation, resources, access, safety, time, coordination, and an effective operator as sufficient evidence for changed feedback.

`TopologyChangeCriteria` distinguishes changed state, changed feedback, changed transition system, and changed topology. Topology promotion requires explicit changes to neighbourhoods, connectivity, and admissible paths rather than a changed state alone.

## Closure, refusal, repair, and residuals

- `ClosureKinds` distinguishes epistemic, practical, relational, political, and administrative closure under a declared grammar.
- `ClosureInteractionLaws` blocks automatic promotion between closure kinds and records unresolved residuals as barriers to epistemic closure.
- `JustifiedRefusal` prevents relationality becoming compulsory reconciliation.
- `RepairCalculus` treats repair as a staged transformation rather than return to an untouched past.
- `RepairAdmissibility` requires cessation before restitution, makes trust restoration separate, permits coexistence as a terminal repair target, and lets irreparable loss modify later obligations.
- `ResidualStatus` classifies residuals before policy selection.
- `ResidualCompositionOrder` tracks residual amplification and preservation through composition.

## Provenance and application

`CulturalProvenanceAdapter` reuses `DASHI.Culture.CulturalProvenanceBoundaryCore`. `CulturalProvenanceInstances` adds candidate rows for pattern-mind, reciprocity, yarning, the user-supplied Maori plant-teaching example, and relational-law language. Every row retains limits, local-context requirements, and non-representation boundaries.

`MentalHealthWorkedExample` keeps bodily, developmental, relational, material, symbolic, and existential dimensions live.

`MentalHealthEvidenceAdapter` separates:

```text
candidate interpretation
-> evidence-supported interpretation
-> actionable support plan
-> separately governed clinical claim
```

It reuses the systemic-distress governance surface and relational-chart residual receipts. Consent, safety, accessibility, revision, and separate clinical authority remain mandatory.

## Architecture

`DASHI.Philosophy.StableCore` exposes reusable constitutional primitives, categories, orders, composition laws, closure interactions, repair admissibility, and topology criteria.

`DASHI.Philosophy.Applications` exposes separately gated cultural and empirical instances.

`DASHI.EverythingPhilosophyClosure` imports the repository aggregate, stable core, applications, and regression surfaces without forcing application-specific authority into the ordinary core.
