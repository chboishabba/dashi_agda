# Constructive TOE Framing Layer

## Replacement Abstract

DASHI is best framed here as a constrained constructive physics-unification
program, not as a completed theory of everything and not as a governance-only
paper. The paper presents the current typed closure frontier of that program:
every promoted statement must be tied to an inhabited theorem, receipt, or
audit surface at its canonical definition site. Missing constructors, missing
authority tokens, blocked promotion routes, and request-only modules are not
editorial gaps to be hidden. They are first-class typed objects that determine
the boundary of the paper's admissible physics claims.

The central thesis is constructive physics unification under typed obstruction
semantics. DASHI's current value is not that it has solved empirical
validation, non-flat GR, Schwarzschild recovery, upstream E8 completion,
strict Drell-Yan authority, a universal G6 route, or completed physical
unification. Its value is that it stages those unification goals as explicit
closure obligations and exposes which steps are inhabited today. The
fail-closed theorem-governance machinery is the enforcement architecture that
prevents adjacent diagnostics or aspirational module names from becoming
physics claims.

The paper therefore reports a bounded inventory: G2 schema adoption for current
core-facing consumers, tracked G6 cross-lane receipt surfaces, selected
finite-support G3 subtraction support, an E8 integer/local-to-upstream
promotion boundary, and a bounded W3 `t43` Drell-Yan comparison-law receipt.
Accepted external empirical authority, GR, stress-energy, strict Drell-Yan
falsification-lane success, upstream E8, and completed-unification claims
remain outside the positive result unless their canonical receipt surfaces
become inhabited in the owning lanes.

## Replacement Introduction

The difficult editorial problem in DASHI is not only technical breadth. It is
claim control. The repository contains theorem surfaces, bridge records,
diagnostics, request packs, promotion audits, authority tokens, and blocker
ledgers across mathematical and physics-facing lanes. Without a disciplined
framing, that vocabulary can make a research program sound like a completed
theory. This paper adopts the opposite rule. A claim is paper-admissible only
when the relevant constructive object is already present at the correct
definition site; otherwise the missing object is named as an obstruction.

This gives DASHI a defensible TOE-facing interpretation. The claim is not that
all physics is completed, externally accepted, or experimentally closed. The
claim is that DASHI stages physics unification as a constructive typed program:
carrier geometry, filtrations, projection-defect structure, empirical-contact
receipts, and obstruction surfaces are handled inside one closure architecture.
Obstruction is not treated as a failure of exposition. It is part of the
formal state.

The resulting contribution is a bounded physics-unification package with an
obstruction-aware enforcement layer. In this layer, a theorem or receipt can
promote a local result only along the route authorized by its owning module. A
request surface can ask for an empirical, geometric, or upstream mathematical
authority, but it does not supply that authority. A diagnostic can motivate a
later bridge, but it does not become validation. A local semantic construction
can clarify a mathematical carrier, but it does not become upstream E8
completion or a broader physics result unless the promotion surface is
inhabited.

This distinction matters for the paper's current positive scope. The G2 lane
may be described as a direction-indexed SFGC schema adoption for current
core-facing consumers. The G6 lane may be described through the official
tracked cross-lane route and its above-threshold consumer. The G3 lane may be
described as selected finite-support constructive subtraction support. The
E8/LILA lane may be described as an integer and local semantic boundary with
upstream promotion still blocked. The Drell-Yan lane may be described as a
bounded W3 `t43` comparison-law receipt, while its stricter `chi2/dof <= 2`
and strict log-covariance protocols remain failed diagnostics. These are
constructive closure surfaces and promotion boundaries, not a solved physics
package.

It also matters for what the paper must not say. The manuscript must not claim
empirical validation, strict Drell-Yan authority acceptance, sourced Einstein
equations, non-flat GR closure, Schwarzschild recovery, continuum recovery,
upstream `E8RootEnumerationComplete` promotion, universal G6 closure, limited
SM+GR closure, GRQFT closure, or full DASHI unification. Those phrases may
appear only as blocked claims, future-work obligations, request surfaces, or
anti-overclaim boundaries.

The paper's strongest formulation is therefore not "DASHI is a completed
theory of everything." It is: DASHI is a fail-closed constructive physics-
unification architecture. The distinction is substantive. It lets the
manuscript discuss TOE-shaped ambition while refusing to convert ambition into
physics completion. Typed governance is the enforcement layer, including the
blockers that prevent premature promotion.

## Framing Commitments

1. Use "constructive TOE architecture" to mean the physics-unification program
   under typed closure, obstruction, and promotion governance.
2. Pair every TOE-facing phrase with an explicit fail-closed boundary.
3. Treat blocked claims as first-class formal artifacts, not as missing prose.
4. Keep empirical, GR, strict DY, upstream E8, universal G6, and full
   unification outside the positive result.
5. Prefer "constructive route", "architecture", "obligation", "receipt",
   "blocker", and "promotion boundary" over "validation", "completion", or
   "solution" unless describing an inhabited local receipt or a blocked/future
   surface.

## Phrases That Still Risk TOE Overclaim

The following phrases should be avoided or immediately qualified if they occur
elsewhere in the manuscript:

1. "DASHI is a theory of everything."
2. "DASHI has completed physics unification."
3. "completed unification theory."
4. "empirically validated DASHI carrier."
5. "Drell-Yan confirms the architecture."
6. "DASHI derives Einstein equations."
7. "Schwarzschild recovered."
8. "E8 completion is established."
9. "universal G6 closure."
10. "strict physical calibration is complete."

Safer replacements are:

1. "DASHI is a constructive physics-unification architecture with fail-closed
   typed promotion boundaries."
2. "The paper advances the physics-unification program by staging its current
   inhabited surfaces and exact remaining obligations without promoting a
   completed unification theory."
3. "The relevant empirical, GR, E8, DY, and full-unification routes remain
   blocked unless their canonical receipts are inhabited."
