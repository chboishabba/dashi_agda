## Introduction Strategy

The introduction should draw only on the outreach archive families that clarify
why Paper 1 is a bounded constructive physics-unification paper with
fail-closed promotion boundaries.

Use `Architectural Closure Status` as the primary introduction-facing archive
family. It reinforces the manuscript's central distinction: the closure core
has meaningful typed surfaces, while completed physics promotion remains far
enough away that the honest paper shape is a bounded constructive-unification
paper. This family should support prose about why the paper exists: to prevent
historical planning language, adjacent physics ambition, and optimistic closure
narratives from being confused with inhabited theorem surfaces.

Use the `cone-monotonicity branch family` only as negative motivation for
fail-closed governance. It can help explain why empirical-looking diagnostics
and fragmented closure premises need a strict promotion boundary. It should not
be presented as empirical support for Paper 1.

Use `Interference and Learning Demo` and `Wave-field Learning with Kernels`
sparingly in the introduction, if at all. Their best introduction role is to
show that DASHI has adjacent concrete benchmark motivations, but the
manuscript should immediately state that those motivations are not part of the
promoted Paper 1 claim set. If space is tight, keep these families out of the
introduction and reserve them for future work.

Do not use `Math Mysticism Breakdown`, moonshine-adjacent language, or
Monster-facing material as an introduction hook for Paper 1. That route risks
making the paper look like it depends on speculative numerology or unpromoted
moonshine claims. If mentioned, it belongs in provenance/related work with a
strict non-dependency warning.

## Related Work and Provenance Strategy

The related-work/provenance section may use the archive ranking to explain how
the current formal-methods target was selected, while preserving the rule that
repo-local typed receipts control the claims.

Use `Branch - Formalism Bridging GR and MDL` and its LES-facing companion as
provenance for gauge, constraint, and geometry questions. Their role is to
explain the question lineage behind future bridge surfaces such as gauge
constraint adapters and non-flat GR requests. They should not be used to claim
that matter, gauge, or GR closure is complete.

Use `Branch - Topology and MDA/MDL` as broad background for the wave/topology
vocabulary only if the manuscript needs to account for why psi or wave-lift
language appears in repository history. It should be explicitly classified as
background vocabulary, not as a Paper 1 proof ingredient.

Use `Branch - Math Mysticism Breakdown` as a boundary-management reference:
it is useful because it separates serious graded-series/module questions from
claims that would read as numerological. In Paper 1, this family should support
exclusion language, not positive theorem claims.

Use implementation-lane families such as `Filament Fining Implementation` and
`Spectral Gradient Implementation` only if a provenance appendix needs to
explain lane separation. They are not needed for the main paper argument and
should not appear in the core introduction.

## Future-Work Strategy

The future-work section can use the archive families more freely, provided
each item is framed as a theorem-shape or research queue rather than a present
result.

Wave and kernel families should inform future work on concrete benchmarks,
interference experiments, sparse wave reconstruction, and kernel learning.
They can be attached to later empirical or computational papers, not to Paper
1's promoted formal core.

Topology, MDA/MDL, wave-lift, graded-series, and moonshine-adjacent families
should inform future work only as staged mathematical questions: What typed
bridge would connect these structures to a DASHI claim surface? What trace,
graded-module, or carrier theorem would be needed? What would count as a
negative result? Until those typed bridges exist, these families remain
development context.

Formalism-bridging GR and MDL families should inform the Paper 4 queue already
described in the manuscript: non-flat connection adapters, curvature, Ricci
contraction, Bianchi witnesses, stress-energy interfaces, Schwarzschild
matching, and continuum recovery. They should strengthen the future-work map
without changing Paper 1's claim ledger.

Cone-monotonicity families should inform future empirical-governance work:
premise diagnostics, consolidation of branch-fragmented evidence, and authority
token requirements. They should not be folded into Paper 1 as validation.

## Exclusions From Paper 1

Exclude the following outreach-ranked uses from Paper 1 to avoid scope drift:

1. Any claim that wave/interference demos validate the formal carrier.
2. Any claim that kernel-learning experiments close G3, empirical, or physical
   adequacy gates.
3. Any claim that topology, MDA/MDL, or psi language supplies a theorem bridge
   without a repo-local receipt.
4. Any claim that wave-lift, graded-series, B4 shell series, twined traces, JMD,
   Monster, or moonshine material is required for the Paper 1 result.
5. Any claim that gauge/constraint/GR bridge discussion proves matter coupling,
   non-flat GR, sourced Einstein equations, Schwarzschild recovery, continuum
   recovery, GRQFT closure, or limited SM+GR closure.
6. Any claim that cone-monotonicity or empirical-cone branches provide accepted
   empirical authority.
7. Any implementation-lane detail whose main purpose is CPU/GPU parity,
   simulation logistics, or spectral-gradient engineering.
8. Any archive thread family used as a theorem citation, bibliographic
   substitute, or replacement for current Agda receipt surfaces.

The manuscript should keep the archive families subordinate to the current
claim ledger. If a sentence cannot be rewritten as provenance, motivation,
exclusion, or future-work staging, it probably does not belong in Paper 1.
