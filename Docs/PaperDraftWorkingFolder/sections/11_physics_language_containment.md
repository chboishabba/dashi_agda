## Containment Rule

Paper 1 may use empirical and GR material as physics-unification lanes whose
current closure status is bounded. The sections may show how DASHI names
receipts, records diagnostics, exposes missing authority, and prevents claim
promotion. They must not say that the present paper establishes empirical
validation, sourced GR, a Schwarzschild limit, stress-energy construction, an
Einstein tensor, strict Drell-Yan success, or completed physical unification.

The constructive TOE framing is the paper's physics framing: DASHI is
constructing a typed route by which such claims could later become checkable.
The current paper is not the promotion event for every downstream physics
claim.

## Safe Framing Patterns

- Say "physics-unification closure lane", not "completed physics result".
- Say "diagnostic", "request surface", "receipt boundary", or "future-work
  interface", not "validation", "derivation", or "recovery".
- Say "records bounded empirical contact under fail-closed promotion", not
  "confirms the model against data".
- Say "constructive TOE architecture with blocked downstream closures", not
  "completed TOE".
- Say "blocked pending accepted authority", not "nearly accepted" or
  "effectively validated".
- Say "the current paper records the obstruction", not "the current paper
  solves the physical problem".

## Editorial Scan Rules

When revising `Paper1_Manuscript.md`, `sections/05_empirical_appendix.md`, or
`sections/06_gr_future_work.md`, scan every occurrence of these terms:

- empirical validation
- validates
- accepted evidence
- strict Drell-Yan
- W4 adequacy
- stress-energy
- Einstein tensor
- Einstein equation
- sourced GR
- Schwarzschild
- continuum recovery
- GRQFT closure
- unification

Each occurrence should either be explicitly blocked, explicitly framed as a
future-work/request path, or removed. Positive wording is admissible only when
it names an inhabited receipt supplied by the owning lane.
