# 2. Governance and Fail-Closed Method

This section states the paper's governance rule. It is a manuscript rule for
claim selection, not a promotion mechanism. The paper treats an asserted result
as admissible only when it is tied to an exact typed theorem, receipt, token, or
obstruction surface at its canonical definition site. A surface that is
request-only, diagnostic-only, template-only, locally shadowed, postulated, or
constructorless remains a blocker even when the surrounding prose, public
sources, or exploratory calculations are informative.

The governing status documents for this rule are
`Docs/CurrentGateStatus.md`, `Docs/LimitedSMGRPaperReadinessMatrix.md`, and
`Docs/Paper1DraftSkeleton.md`. They are documentation control surfaces: they
record which claims are admissible for this paper, which typed artifacts must be
cited, and which authority gates remain closed. They do not themselves construct
Agda values, add constructors, accept provider packets, or close any token.

## Typed Receipts as Claim Boundaries

The paper uses typed receipts as the boundary between an allowed statement and
an excluded statement. A positive claim may be made only when the corresponding
Agda-facing theorem or receipt is already inhabited, and the claim is limited to
the exact scope of that type. A blocked claim may be discussed only as an
obstruction, request, diagnostic, or remaining obligation.

This rule has two practical consequences for the manuscript.

First, the paper can cite inhabited internal surfaces such as the current G2
direction-indexed schema-extension receipt, the official tracked G6 commuting
theorem, tracked G6 above-threshold consumers, and the constructive finite-
support G3 subtraction-support route. Those claims must remain bounded to their
typed statements.

Second, the paper cannot promote an external, empirical, physical, GR, GRQFT,
or unification claim merely because adjacent evidence exists. If the required
authority token or receipt is absent, the manuscript must name the exact missing
artifact and preserve the claim as forbidden.

## Canonical Definition Sites

Authority is recognized only at canonical definition sites. A token or receipt
type is controlled by the module that defines it, not by a downstream mirror,
paper table, request template, or locally convenient replacement. Therefore a
claim can close only when the canonical type is inhabited by a real constructor,
an accepted non-postulated exact hook, or an external provider/governance value
that has the required type.

For the current paper scope, the important canonical authority boundaries are:

- W2:
  `DASHI.Physics.Closure.NaturalP2ConvergencePromotionObligation.NaturalP2ConvergencePromotionAuthorityToken`.
- W3:
  `DASHI.Physics.Closure.W3AcceptedEmpiricalAuthorityGate.W3AcceptedEvidenceAuthorityToken`.
- W4:
  `DASHI.Physics.Closure.W4ZAdequacyReceipt.AcceptedDYLuminosityConventionAuthority`.

The current status records say that these authority types remain
constructorless at their canonical sites. No accepted non-postulated exact
policy hook is present there. Consequently, W2, W3, and W4 remain fail-closed.
The same paper boundary extends to W5 and downstream GRQFT consumers: no W5
correction, accepted DY convention closure, GRQFT promotion, or limited
SM+GR/full-unification claim is admissible without the corresponding canonical
receipt or authority payload.

## Constructorless Tokens

A constructorless authority token is not partial evidence. It is an explicit
closed gate. The paper may cite such a token type to explain why a result is
blocked, but it may not describe the gate as pending-but-effectively-closed or
closed by implication.

This is why the W2/W3/W4 posture is fail-closed:

- W2 still lacks an inhabitant of
  `NaturalP2ConvergencePromotionAuthorityToken`.
- W3 still lacks an inhabitant of `W3AcceptedEvidenceAuthorityToken`.
- W4 still lacks an inhabitant of
  `AcceptedDYLuminosityConventionAuthority`.
- W5 and GRQFT remain downstream of missing accepted DY convention, W4/W5
  authority, physical calibration, matter/stress-energy, empirical validation,
  and promotion receipts.

The paper may describe these as named blockers. It must not present them as
accepted authority, empirical validation, physical calibration, sourced GR, or
unification evidence.

## Rejected Closure Routes

The governance rule rejects several local shortcuts. These routes are
inadmissible even if they would make prose or downstream files easier to write.

- A local shadow record with fields resembling the canonical token does not
  inhabit the canonical token type.
- A local postulate does not constitute accepted authority.
- A postulate-to-record rewrite does not close a constructorless token unless
  the owner/governance boundary explicitly approves a real constructor or exact
  hook at the canonical site.
- A request template, provider-response skeleton, or hook request is not an
  accepted response.
- Public-source evidence, CMS/HEPData metadata, CT18/LHAPDF packets,
  exploratory numeric agreement, or self-issued public-evidence summaries do
  not by themselves construct W3, W4, W5, physical-calibration, or GRQFT
  authority.
- Record conversion or field-level resemblance does not turn a self-issued
  value into public accepted evidence or a canonical token.

These rejected routes are part of the method, not editorial caution. They keep
the manuscript from laundering candidate evidence into a typed authority claim.

## Authority Gates

The authority gates are asymmetric. An inhabited theorem surface can support a
bounded constructive-unification claim, but it cannot close an external
authority gate unless its type explicitly supplies that authority. Conversely, an
external provider packet can be recorded as provenance or a request response,
but it cannot support a paper claim until the canonical acceptance boundary
ingests it as the required typed token or receipt.

For this paper, the admissible positive package is therefore internal and
bounded:

- G2: direction-indexed SFGC schema adoption for the current core-facing
  consumers.
- G6: official tracked cross-lane commuting and tracked above-threshold
  coordinate independence, on the tracked route only.
- G3: constructive finite-support subtraction support for the selected finite-
  support subtype.
- E8: integer/local semantic completeness and the upstream promotion boundary,
  without upstream `E8RootEnumerationComplete` promotion.

All stronger language remains outside the claim set. In particular, the paper
does not claim W2 natural p2-convergence promotion, W3 accepted empirical
authority, W4 Z adequacy, W5 correction, accepted DY convention closure,
Candidate256 physical calibration, matter or stress-energy construction,
sourced non-flat GR, Schwarzschild recovery, continuum scaling, GRQFT closure,
limited SM+GR, old full G6 `LaneOperator` promotion, upstream E8 completion, or
full unification.

## Paper-Scope Governance

The paper's governance contribution is admissible only as a fail-closed
packaging method. It may say that the manuscript filters claims through typed
receipts, canonical definition sites, and explicit obstruction ledgers. It may
also say that constructorless tokens and request-only surfaces are useful
because they make missing authority machine-checkable and auditable.

The paper must not say that governance has supplied the missing authority. The
status documents and this section do not alter Agda modules, provide
constructors, validate provider packets, or satisfy downstream physical
obligations. Their role is to prevent overclaiming: a paper claim is included
only when the corresponding typed surface is inhabited, and every missing token
or receipt remains visible as a blocker.
