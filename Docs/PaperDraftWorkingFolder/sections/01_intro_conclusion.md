# Title

Fail-Closed Constructive Physics Unification With Typed Obstruction Semantics

# Abstract

This paper presents DASHI as a narrow constructive physics-unification program
extracted from the DASHI Agda development. Its purpose is to state the current
inhabited closure frontier without promoting uninhabited physical claims. The
paper uses a fail-closed rule: a positive statement is admissible only when it
is tied to an inhabited theorem or receipt surface at the relevant definition
site; request-only modules, constructorless authority types, local shadows, and
postulated hooks are recorded as blockers.

The positive inventory is deliberately bounded. It includes direction-indexed
G2 SFGC schema adoption for current core-facing consumers, tracked G6
cross-lane commuting and above-threshold coordinate independence on the
official `GL.FactorVec` route, constructive finite-support G3 subtraction
support for selected operators, and an E8 integer/local-to-upstream promotion
boundary. The paper is a physics-unification paper, but not a completed
unification paper: it does not claim empirical authority closure, sourced GR,
Schwarzschild recovery, continuum recovery, GRQFT closure, or full physical
closure.

# Introduction

DASHI contains a growing set of typed theorem surfaces, receipt records, and
request or obstruction ledgers for a physics-unification program. That scale
creates a manuscript problem: prose can easily overstate what has been
formally inhabited. This paper addresses that problem by making the
unification frontier explicit. The contribution is a constructive physics-
unification paper: it describes which closure claims are currently supported by
exact Agda surfaces and which adjacent claims remain blocked by missing
constructors, missing payloads, or request-only authority boundaries.

The paper is intentionally narrow. It does not argue from diagnostics,
aspirational module names, or local prose to downstream scientific promotion.
Instead, each positive statement is tied to a named theorem or receipt surface.
For G2, the admissible claim is that direction-indexed SFGC schema adoption is
complete for the current core-facing consumers, as witnessed by
`DASHI.Physics.Closure.G2SFGCGaugeFieldSchemaExtension.canonicalG2SFGCGaugeFieldSchemaExtensionReceipt`
and `noRemainingG2SFGCSchemaExtensionMissing`. This is a schema-extension
claim, not a field-equation or unification claim.

For G6, the admissible route is the official tracked route. The paper may cite
`DASHI.Physics.Closure.G6CrossLaneCommutingTheorem.canonicalG6OfficialTrackedCrossLaneCommutingTheorem`
and
`DASHI.Physics.Closure.G6AboveThresholdIndependence.canonicalG6OfficialTrackedAboveThresholdCoordinateIndependence`
for tracked cross-lane commuting and above-threshold coordinate independence.
It must also retain the old-interface obstruction: the legacy `LaneOperator`
surface remains blocked by the universal vanished-prime identity law recorded
by `g6LaneOperatorUniversalVanishesIdentityObstruction` and the corresponding
old-route obstruction.

For G3, the admissible positive statement is constructive and subtype-bounded.
The paper may report certified subtraction support for constructive
finite-support selected operators via
`selectedFiniteSupportOperatorSubtractionCertifiedSupport`,
`selectedFiniteSupportOperatorSubtractionSupportWitnessFromCertified`, and
`G3SelectedSubtractionCertifiedSupport`. It may not generalize that support to
arbitrary `WaveFunctionOperator` values or to global Schrodinger promotion.
The remaining valuation and norm-law requirements are blockers, including the
surface named by `exactRemainingDifferenceNormLawBlockers` and the first
missing norm-index law
`missingSelectedOperatorDifferenceNormIndexLaws`.

For E8, the paper may describe integer completeness and the local semantic
finite boundary while preserving upstream ownership. The admissible surfaces
are
`DASHI.Algebra.Trit.E8RootEnumeration.integerIndexedRootsCompleteForTwoSparseShapeTheorem`,
`DASHI.Algebra.Trit.E8RootEnumeration.canonicalE8RootEnumerationCompletePromotionBoundary`,
and
`DASHI.Physics.Closure.LilaE8RootEnumerationNoDuplicatesSurface.canonicalE8UpstreamCompleteReceiptPromotionAudit`.
The upstream complete receipt remains blocked until the upstream owner supplies
a legitimate constructor or promotion API after the remaining proof and payload
obligations are discharged.

The organizing principle is constructive unification under fail-closed
governance. Constructors, receipts, and tokens count only at canonical
definition sites. Request templates, public evidence summaries, local shadow
records, and postulate-to-record rewrites do not by themselves promote a
claim. This rule is not the paper's main thesis; it is the enforcement
architecture by which the physics-unification thesis remains theorem-backed
rather than rhetorical.

# Conclusion

The result is a paper-ready constructive physics-unification package, not a
completed physics package. Its positive claims are limited to
exact theorem and receipt surfaces for G2 schema adoption, tracked G6 commuting
and above-threshold independence, constructive finite-support G3 subtraction
support, and the E8 integer/local-to-upstream boundary. Its negative claims are
equally important: request-only authority surfaces, constructorless authority
tokens, old full `LaneOperator` promotion, G3 norm-law closure, upstream E8
completion, and external physics-facing promotion surfaces remain blocked.

This fail-closed presentation gives readers a precise audit trail. It states
what is inhabited, identifies what is merely requested or obstructed, and keeps
the manuscript boundary inside the current formal evidence. The paper therefore
serves as a physics-unification manuscript with typed claim control, not as a
claim of completed unification, empirical authority closure, sourced GR,
Schwarzschild recovery, continuum recovery, GRQFT closure, or limited SM+GR
closure.
