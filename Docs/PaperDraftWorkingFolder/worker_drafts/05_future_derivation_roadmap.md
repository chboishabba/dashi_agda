# Paper 1 Future-Derivation Roadmap

Status: Worker 5 draft; docs-only; non-promoting future-work map.

Paper 1 establishes a derivation grammar. It does not establish all of the
downstream proofs named in this roadmap. The paper's positive contribution is
the typed discipline by which a carrier surface, a promoted projection, a
visible defect, a refinement level, a transport law, and an authority receipt
can be composed without silently upgrading an open problem into a result.

Accordingly, the future lanes below are not a catalog of solved physics or a
claim that DASHI already recovers every named domain. They are derivation
targets opened by three controlled operations:

1. refinement: increasing address depth, filtration resolution, empirical
   resolution, or semantic precision while preserving typed compatibility;
2. projection: exposing an admitted surface while retaining the residual
   defect left by that exposure;
3. transport: moving structure across lanes, gauges, quotients, observations,
   or semantic interfaces only when the required commuting, invariance, and
   authority receipts are inhabited.

The map is therefore grammatical rather than promotional. A later paper may
promote a lane only by inhabiting the required adapter, theorem, comparison,
or authority surface. Paper 1 records how those obligations are to be stated.

## Base Grammar

The common future-work shape is:

`carrier refinement -> admissible projection -> residual defect ->
transport/adaptation obligation -> receipt-gated promotion`

This grammar lets Paper 1 say why a downstream question is well-formed without
saying that the downstream proof has already been supplied. Each target lane
must identify:

- the carrier or observation surface being refined;
- the projection made visible to the theorem or empirical comparison;
- the defect that remains after projection;
- the transport or adapter needed to compare with another lane;
- the receipt whose absence blocks promotion.

Where any item is missing, the lane remains future work.

## Promotion Discipline

The roadmap should be read in dependency order, not as a list of advertised
achievements. Gauge-specific physics depends on promoted field carriers, not
on field-like notation. QFT depends on promoted operator and observable laws,
not on finite-support bookkeeping alone. GR depends on a non-flat connection
and sourced stress-energy interface, not on flat finite-difference
commutation. Black holes and cosmology depend on GR and matter interfaces.
Biology, cognition, and language depend on domain data, calibration,
observation, and semantic transport, not on analogy with projection alone.

For each later derivation, the required proof form is:

1. state the target surface;
2. state the refinement/projection/transport chain;
3. state the residual defect at each step;
4. state the exact receipt required for promotion;
5. refuse the promoted claim until that receipt is inhabited.

This is the sense in which Paper 1 is foundational for future work. It fixes
the grammar for disciplined derivation across physical and semantic lanes, but
it does not perform every derivation that the grammar makes expressible.

## Manuscript Boundary Paragraph

Paper 1 should describe the future program as follows:

> The present paper supplies a typed derivation grammar rather than a completed
> derivation of every downstream domain. General relativity, QFT, gauge theory,
> the electromagnetic, weak, and strong interactions, black holes, cosmology,
> quantum phenomena, information/entropy, biology, cognition, and language are
> future lanes only insofar as they can be stated through refinement,
> projection, transport, residual-defect accounting, and receipt-gated
> promotion. The paper's claim is that this grammar makes such obligations
> explicit and fail-closed; it is not that the present manuscript proves all of
> those theories.

## Exclusion Rule

Do not convert this roadmap into hype language. Avoid phrases such as "DASHI
explains everything," "recovers all physics," "derives life and mind," or
"solves unification." The admissible phrasing is narrower: Paper 1 defines a
typed architecture in which future derivations must pass through explicit
refinement, projection, transport, defect, and receipt gates.
