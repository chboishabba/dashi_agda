## Worm, soil, noodles, pants, and braids

The positive object is the realised support or **worm**. The negative object is
its soil, latent complement, or untraversed medium. Negative does not mean only
forbidden: cells may be latent, unresolved, unobserved, incompatible, or
collapsed.

A pair of pants is a local thick-tube split or merge. Recursive pants may form
rhizomatic networks with reconnection and loops. A dry bundle of spaghetti is
only a collection of candidate strands. It becomes sheaf-like when sections
have restrictions, overlap comparisons, and gluing laws.

Braiding requires an admissible deformation class. Flexibility is represented
as the size and laws of that class: strands may change relative position while
retaining identity and avoiding illegal collision. A full braid-group action,
isotopy theorem, and arbitrary sheaf gluing theorem remain unpromoted.
