# Robin Wall Kimmerer acknowledgement

DASHI's recurring use of **braids**, **dialectical braids**, reciprocal knowledge,
provenance-preserving conjunction, and obligation-bearing knowing was materially
inspired by Robin Wall Kimmerer's writing, especially *Braiding Sweetgrass*.

The influence is not limited to the word “braid.”  It includes the discipline of
holding distinct knowledge practices together without flattening them into one
master vocabulary; treating reciprocity and obligation as part of knowing; and
keeping person, community, place, land, history, and practice in relation.

This acknowledgement applies particularly to:

- dialectical braids that preserve contention rather than average it away;
- knowledge strands whose provenance, methods, warrants, purposes, and
  obligations remain explicit;
- reciprocal correction without forced fusion;
- anti-extractive boundaries around cultural and relational material;
- the claim that knowing can create obligations, not merely classifications.

## Boundary of the acknowledgement

DASHI does **not** claim to formalise Indigenous knowledge, Anishinaabe knowledge,
or living Indigenous practice.  A typed record for provenance, reciprocity, or
obligation is not a substitute for community authority, language, land,
relationship, ceremony, consent, or accountable participation.

The formal modules record an influence and constrain DASHI's own conduct.  They
do not grant permission to extract teachings, universalise them, convert them
into empirical variables, or present DASHI as an Indigenous knowledge system.

The corresponding formal acknowledgement is:

- `DASHI.Culture.KimmererBraidingAcknowledgement`
- `DASHI.Culture.CulturalProvenanceBoundaryCore`

The braided-emotion tranche imports this acknowledgement through
`DASHI.Biology.BraidedEmotionCanonicalIntegration`.
