# Section 5. Candidate Gauge Group Origin

This section records the current gauge-group reading as a candidate ledger.  It
does not derive the Standard Model gauge group.  The admissible claim is that
the Heegner and prime-lane data single out three natural symmetry sources whose
labels match the roles of \(U(1)_Y\), \(SU(2)_L\), and \(SU(3)_c\), while the
promotion from candidate labels to physical gauge reconstruction remains open.

## 5.1 Label Convention

We use three labels.

| Label | Meaning in this section |
| --- | --- |
| Derived finite structure | A finite arithmetic or carrier feature already recorded by the receipt stack. |
| Candidate gauge origin | A symmetry interpretation naturally suggested by that finite structure. |
| Open promotion | A missing theorem or external authority needed before identifying the candidate with the physical Standard Model gauge factor. |

The following subsections use the second label deliberately.  They are
candidate origins, not derivations.

## 5.2 The \(U(1)_Y\) Candidate

The hypercharge candidate is the Hecke-circle extension of the \(X_0(3)\)
CM stabilizer.  At the finite level, the \(p=3\) lane carries the
\(\mathbb Z/6\) CM stabilizer.  This gives the correct arithmetic unit for
hypercharge bookkeeping: Standard Model hypercharges are integral multiples of
\(1/6\), and electric charge sees the \(\mathbb Z/3\) quotient scale after the
usual weak-isospin contribution is included.

The candidate reading is therefore:

\[
  \mathbb Z/6 \subset \mathrm{Stab}_{CM}(X_0(3))
  \quad \leadsto \quad
  U(1)_Y .
\]

The arrow is not a theorem of gauge reconstruction.  It denotes the proposed
continuous Hecke-circle completion of the finite CM character.  The current
receipts support the arithmetic quantisation pattern and the \(X_0(3)\)
normalisation role; they do not yet supply a DHR/DR compact group theorem, a
physical \(U(1)_Y\) principal bundle, or an external QFT authority binding this
circle to the Standard Model hypercharge gauge field.

## 5.3 The \(SU(2)_L\) Candidate

The weak-isospin candidate is the \(SU(2)_3\) Chern-Simons/WZW factor appearing
in the level-flow picture.  Its role in the present paper is representation
bookkeeping: the matter lanes require a two-state weak splitting, and the
candidate \(SU(2)\) source supplies the natural doublet label.

The important caveat is decoupling.  Level-rank heuristics can make
\(SU(2)_3\) and \(SU(3)_2\)-type structures appear adjacent.  Paper 8 does not
identify this adjacency with a single unified gauge factor.  The intended
separation is by the lepton-quark split: lepton and quark sectors are treated
as different symmetry-enriched topological phases, so their \(SU(2)\)- and
\(SU(3)\)-like readings are SET-inequivalent.  Under that interpretation,
\(SU(2)_3\) supplies weak-isospin representation data while the color candidate
belongs to a different sectoral enrichment.

Thus the present claim is:

\[
  SU(2)_3\ \text{CS/WZW data}
  \quad \leadsto \quad
  SU(2)_L\ \text{candidate representation label},
\]

with decoupling from color imposed by the lepton-quark SET split.  A formal
proof that this split forbids unwanted \(SU(2)\)-\(SU(3)\) mixing is still
pending.

## 5.4 The \(SU(3)_c\) Candidate

The color candidate comes from the three-lane chiral-limit degeneracy.  In the
finite carrier, the first three quark lanes form a triplet-like structure.  In
the chiral limit, where the lane masses are not yet distinguished by physical
Yukawa calibration, this degeneracy admits a continuous special-unitary
completion:

\[
  \text{three quark lanes in the chiral limit}
  \quad \leadsto \quad
  SU(3)_c .
\]

This is the same kind of candidate lift as the triadic carrier note: three
latent sectors plus an invariant Hermitian form and determinant-one admissible
mixing give an \(SU(3)\)-like internal symmetry at carrier level.  The paper
does not claim that ternary counting alone derives the Lie algebra
\(\mathfrak{su}(3)\), Yang-Mills dynamics, confinement, or QCD color as an
accepted physical gauge theory.

## 5.5 Anomaly Status

Anomaly cancellation is not independently derived here.  The present gauge
assignment inherits anomaly cancellation only conditionally from the Standard
Model charge assignments it is trying to match.  In other words, if the
candidate representation table is externally bound to the usual Standard Model
fermion content and hypercharges, then the usual Standard Model anomaly
cancellations are inherited.

This is not a DASHI proof of anomaly cancellation from first principles.  The
missing promotion is an internal representation theorem that derives the exact
fermion charges and chiralities, followed by a formal anomaly calculation
inside the carrier/DHR interface.

## 5.6 Section Claim

The section supplies a labelled candidate origin for the three Standard Model
gauge factors:

| Gauge factor | Candidate source | Current status |
| --- | --- | --- |
| \(U(1)_Y\) | Hecke circle on \(X_0(3)\) extending the \(\mathbb Z/6\) CM stabilizer | Candidate; finite charge unit recorded, physical hypercharge authority open |
| \(SU(2)_L\) | \(SU(2)_3\) CS/WZW weak-isospin label | Candidate; decoupling from color requires formal SET-inequivalence proof |
| \(SU(3)_c\) | Three-lane chiral-limit degeneracy and determinant-one triplet mixing | Candidate; QCD dynamics and continuum gauge theory open |
| Anomaly cancellation | Standard Model representation inheritance | Conditional; not first-principles anomaly derivation |

Consequently Paper 8 may say that the arithmetic tower points to the Standard
Model gauge labels.  It must not say that the tower derives
\(U(1)_Y \times SU(2)_L \times SU(3)_c\) as a physical gauge group.
