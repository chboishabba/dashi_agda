# Paper 7 Terminal Composition Draft: Terminal Composition And Blocker Map

Date: `2026-05-29`
Status: standalone draft; docs-only; non-constructive; fail-closed
Scope: terminal composition remains non-constructible in the current repository

## Abstract

This paper states the terminal composition target for DASHI as a typed
obligation and blocker map rather than as a completed theorem.  The intended
terminal product would require the simultaneous promotion of the
carrier-to-physics functor, Yang-Mills spectral and field-equation lanes,
non-flat GR and stress-energy lanes, Hilbert/Stone physical phase-space lanes,
DHR/DR Standard Model reconstruction, Yukawa/CKM physical calibration,
empirical authority gates, and all cross-gate compatibility laws.  The current
repository does not construct that product receipt, and this paper does not
claim unification.

The contribution is a formal account of what a terminal composition would have
to require in DASHI: a terminal composition is admissible only when each component
receipt is inhabited at theorem scope, each continuum or authority boundary is
closed by an explicit typed receipt, and the cross-gate product contains no
unresolved incompatibility.  Existing finite, local, conditional, and
diagnostic receipts are valuable because they identify exact obligations and
prevent silent promotion.  This draft therefore records the architecture and
blocker map for a future terminal composition while explicitly preserving the
current non-promotion state.  Its positive contribution is proof governance:
it makes the terminal claim auditable by showing which receipts are finite,
which are conditional, which are diagnostic, and which blockers still prevent a
terminal product.

## 1. Introduction

DASHI contains many local physics-facing receipt surfaces.  Some receipts
record finite carrier witnesses.  Some record conditional reconstruction
targets.  Some record empirical diagnostic or comparison surfaces.  Some
record negative promotion facts: a continuum theorem is not discharged, an
external authority is absent, or a physical interpretation has not been
constructed.  A terminal composition paper must not flatten those distinctions.

The target of Paper 7 is the end of the composition stack: what would it take
for the repository to claim a unified physical closure theorem?  The answer is
not "a long list of promising receipts."  The answer is a product receipt whose
components are all promoted at the required scope and whose compatibility
interfaces are all inhabited.  Until that product exists, terminal composition
is a typed obligation map.

This draft uses the term terminal composition for the strongest intended
claim: a repository-internal construction that combines the core finite
carrier, Yang-Mills, GR, Hilbert/Stone, DHR/Standard Model, Yukawa/CKM, and
empirical lanes into one coherent promoted receipt.  The current state falls
short of that target.  It has useful finite and conditional evidence, but the
terminal product remains non-constructible.

The word "terminal" should not be read as "completed".  In this paper it
means "the last product that would have to be inhabited before a terminal
claim could be made."  The current object is the map of that product's missing
factors.

## 2. Receipt And Composition Architecture

The terminal composition target should be represented as a product of typed
lanes, not as prose aggregation.  Each lane has three parts:

1. A positive receipt surface recording what has actually been constructed.
2. A boundary receipt recording what kind of promotion would be needed next.
3. A false or blocked terminal field preventing accidental escalation.

At a high level, the terminal product would require the following components.

| Lane | Required promoted content | Current honest role |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Carrier-to-physics functor | exact physical carrier category, object and morphism semantics, functor laws, naturality, filtered-colimit preservation | obligation surface |
| Yang-Mills spectral gap | physical Hilbert space, self-adjoint Hamiltonian, non-vacuum lower bound, continuum construction, Clay authority | finite/local and Clay-blocked tower evidence |
| Yang-Mills field equation | nonabelian connection, curvature, Hodge/pairing, variation, source law, `D * F = J` | finite and selected-surface scaffolding |
| GR and stress-energy | non-flat metric chain, Ricci/scalar/Einstein tensor, contracted Bianchi, W4 matter interface, sourced Einstein equation | boundary and authority receipts |
| Hilbert/Stone phase space | noncollapsed quotient, inner product, completion, strong continuity, self-adjoint Stone generator | finite bridge receipts, physical promotion false |
| DHR/DR reconstruction | arbitrary sectors, tensor category, braiding/statistics, compact group reconstruction, exact `G_DHR` to Standard Model match | finite prime-lane and conditional authority receipts |
| Yukawa/CKM calibration | physical Yukawa matrices, DHR intertwiners, basis changes, CKM field closure, calibrated mass and coupling authority | symbolic targets and diagnostics |
| Empirical authority | frozen artifacts, checksums, covariance/residual receipts, accepted authority labels, no posterior tuning | diagnostic and comparison surfaces |
| Cross-gate compatibility | product laws showing no conflict between promoted lanes | partial consistency surfaces only |

The important architectural rule is monotonicity of claim strength.  A finite
receipt can feed a terminal product only through an explicit promotion receipt.
A conditional receipt can feed a terminal product only after its condition is
inhabited or accepted at the required authority boundary.  An empirical target
can feed a terminal product only after the data, checksum, residual, and
authority receipts are bound.  No lane may be promoted by naming another lane's
analogy.

## 2.1 Composition Status Classes

Every lane in Paper 7 should be assigned one of four statuses.

| Status | Meaning | Terminal use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| finite | inhabited only on a finite carrier, finite depth, selected row, or bounded table | may provide input data, but cannot discharge continuum or physical scope |
| conditional | depends on an explicit external authority or missing construction | may be cited only with its condition attached |
| diagnostic | compares, targets, or classifies empirical or phenomenological data | may motivate future tests, but cannot validate a physical theorem |
| promoted | has the theorem-scope receipt needed by the terminal product | may enter the terminal product directly |

The current terminal composition has no complete promoted product.  The paper's
main table should therefore avoid a binary done/not-done presentation.  The
important information is which status each lane has and which blocker would
move it to the next status.

These blockers are not defects in the draft.  They are the draft's central

named precisely enough that future work can replace them with receipts.

## 4.1 Gate-by-Gate Blocker Narrative

**Gate 1, carrier semantics.**  The repository contains many carrier-level
witnesses, but Paper 7 needs a promoted carrier-to-physics functor with object
mapping, morphism mapping, naturality, compatibility with finite-depth towers,
and preservation laws.  Without that functor, carrier receipts remain typed
local data rather than physical semantics.

**Gate 2, Yang-Mills spectral gap.**  The useful receipts are finite/local
spectral and mass-gap surfaces.  The missing terminal pieces are the physical
Hilbert space, self-adjoint physical Yang-Mills Hamiltonian, non-vacuum lower
bound, uniform continuum construction, and Clay/authority acceptance.  Finite
coercivity cannot be substituted for continuum mass gap.

**Gate 3, Yang-Mills field equation.**  The selected finite curvature and
field-equation scaffolding do not yet promote a real non-flat nonabelian
Yang-Mills equation.  A terminal product needs the selected connection,
curvature, Hodge star, variation pairing, source law, and `D * F = J` route
closed at physical scope.

**Gate 4, GR and stress-energy.**  Contracted-Bianchi and Wald/Friedmann
authority receipts provide important boundaries, but sourced GR remains
blocked by the W4 matter/stress-energy interface and authority-backed
conservation/source law.  Cosmology authority does not promote a DASHI
cosmology theorem or dark-energy conclusion.

**Gate 5, Hilbert/Stone phase space.**  Finite Stone and GNS-style bridges
record useful algebraic structure, but terminal composition needs a
noncollapsed physical quotient, completion, strong continuity, self-adjoint
physical generator, and compatibility with the Yang-Mills Hamiltonian lane.

**Gate 6, DHR/DR and Standard Model gauge.**  Paper 5 supplies finite
prime-lane DHR axiom receipts and a `conditionalOnDRAuthority` status.  It
does not supply arbitrary localized sectors, internal compact group
construction, or full `G_DHR ~= G_SM`.  Terminal composition cannot treat the
conditional target as an unconditional gauge reconstruction.

**Gate 7, matter/Yukawa/CKM.**  Paper 6 supplies carrier-side Yukawa entries,
alpha diagnostics, and a Cabibbo target with
`yukawaSuppressPatternConsistent = true`.  It also records
`cabibboAngleDerived = false` and `physicalCKMPromoted = false`.  Terminal
composition needs physical Yukawa matrices, calibrated Higgs/VEV/W4 authority,
basis changes, non-identity diagonalizers, and CKM/CP promotion.

**Empirical gates.**  Empirical contact surfaces must be separated from
accepted validation.  Terminal use requires frozen artifacts, checksum
binding, residual definitions, covariance/uncertainty models, authority labels,
and a no-retuning protocol.  Diagnostic comparison is not enough.

**Cross-gate product.**  Even if every lane were individually promoted, Paper 7
would still need product laws: shared physical carriers, consistent units,
compatible functors, no double-use of authority boundaries, and commuting
interfaces between GR, Yang-Mills, QFT, matter, and empirical lanes.

## 5. How Existing Receipts Should Be Read

Existing receipts should be read according to their scope labels.

Finite-depth receipts show that a construction is inhabited on a bounded or
depth-indexed carrier.  They do not establish a continuum theorem unless the
continuum lift and authority receipt are also inhabited.

Conditional receipts show that a result follows from an explicitly named
condition, such as accepted DR authority or a missing physical identification.
They do not discharge the condition.

Diagnostic receipts show that a data or prediction lane has a structured
interface to empirical material.  They do not make new-physics claims unless
the artifact, residual, checksum, covariance, and authority gates are all
bound.

False promotion fields are part of the theorem statement.  They are not
editorial caution.  They are the mechanism that keeps partial progress from
becoming a terminal claim by implication.
