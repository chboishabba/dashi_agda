# Section 4 Draft: The Fermion Count and SU(2) Doublet Structure

This section records the count-level matter bookkeeping supplied by the
carrier.  It is a finite receipt surface, not a proof of the full Standard
Model representation category.

Each quark lane is dressed by the `SU(2)_1` boundary/WZW doublet.  The
spin-`1/2` primary has two states, corresponding at count level to the
up-type and down-type members of a weak doublet.  With the colour multiplicity
`N_c = 3`, one quark lane contributes

```text
2 weak-doublet states * 3 colours * 2 chiralities = 12 Weyl states.
```

Across the three quark lanes this gives `36` Weyl quark states.  Each lepton
lane is also doubled by the same weak-doublet bookkeeping into a neutral and
a charged state, with colour multiplicity one:

```text
2 weak-doublet states * 1 colour * 2 chiralities = 4 Weyl states.
```

Across the three lepton lanes this gives `12` Weyl lepton states.  Therefore
the carrier count is

```text
3 * 2 * 3 * 2 + 3 * 2 * 1 * 2 = 36 + 12 = 48.
```

This matches the Standard Model Weyl count only when right-handed neutrinos
are included.  The match is count-level: it does not establish anomaly
cancellation, hypercharge normalisation, physical Yukawa matrices, or exact
left/right representation content.

The colour factor is currently recorded as a candidate.  The three quark
lanes have a discrete `S3` permutation symmetry.  In the chiral limit, where
the lane metric degenerates to an equal-mass form, the continuous extension
candidate is `SU(3)`.  Away from the chiral limit the observed mass hierarchy
breaks this lane symmetry, so the exact emergence of `SU(3)_c` as a physical
gauge factor remains open.

Status: the 48-Weyl count is inhabited as finite bookkeeping with
right-handed neutrinos included.  The `SU(2)_L` doublet, `SU(3)_c` colour,
continuous `U(1)_Y`, exact Standard Model representation, DHR/SM
reconstruction, and all Clay/terminal claims remain unpromoted.
