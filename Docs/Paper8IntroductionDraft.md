# Paper 8 Introduction Draft

DASHI is a fail-closed formal programme for recording arithmetic structures that appear in Standard Model diagnostics without promoting them into physical theorems until every required receipt is inhabited.  The present paper studies the prime-lane Heegner tower generated by the class-number-one discriminants and records which parts of the tower are derivations, which are diagnostics, and which remain open blockers.

The cleanest results are arithmetic and count-level.  The carrier derives the Wolfenstein parameter \(\lambda = \pi\sqrt{3}\alpha_1\) with \(\alpha_1 = \sqrt{m_u/m_c}\), matching the observed value at the \(0.04\%\) level.  It records the Wolfenstein \(A\) diagnostic through the geometric resummation \(14/(1-\alpha_1/2)\), matching at the \(0.05\%\) level while leaving the analytic origin of the resummation as a named problem.  The p3-lane CM unit group \(\mathbb{Z}/6\) gives the hypercharge quantisation unit \(1/6\), and its \(\mathbb{Z}/3\) subgroup records the electric-charge unit \(1/3\).

The Heegner sequence gives the matter-lane skeleton.  The first three lanes, with conductor levels \(3,4,7\), are pairwise coprime and are recorded as the quark-generation lanes.  The next three lanes, \(D=-8,-11,-12\), are recorded as lepton candidates.  Their level overlaps with the quark lanes produce structural quark-lepton Yukawa ratios \(1/2\), \(0\), \(1/3\), and \(1/4\), with \(D=-11\) isolated from the quark levels.  Dressing each matter lane by the SU(2)\(_1\) WZW \(j=1/2\) primary supplies the two isospin states needed for up/down and charged/neutral splitting.  With three colours and two chiralities, the count becomes \(3\cdot2\cdot3\cdot2 + 3\cdot2\cdot1\cdot2 = 48\) Weyl fermions, matching the Standard Model count when right-handed neutrinos are included.

The strongest gauge-sector readings remain candidates.  \(U(1)_Y\) is approached as a continuous extension of the p3-lane \(\mathbb{Z}/6\) character.  \(\mathrm{SU}(2)_L\) is approached through a Chern-Simons/WZW level-flow picture, and \(\mathrm{SU}(3)_c\) through the chiral-limit extension of the three-lane \(S_3\) symmetry to a continuous colour symmetry.  These readings do not yet form a direct product reconstruction of \(U(1)_Y \times \mathrm{SU}(2)_L \times \mathrm{SU}(3)_c\); the SU(2)-SU(3) decoupling from level-rank duality is an explicit open problem.

The paper deliberately does not claim Yang-Mills mass gap, Navier-Stokes regularity, full DHR reconstruction, exact CKM derivation, or terminal unification.  Those claims remain false in the receipt tower.  The contribution is the audited arithmetic skeleton, the positive diagnostics that survive formalisation, and the blocker map that keeps the programme from overclaiming.
