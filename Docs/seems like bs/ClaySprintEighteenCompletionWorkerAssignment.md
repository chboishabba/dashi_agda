# Clay Sprint 18 Completion Worker Assignment

Sprint 18 assigns six concrete worker lanes to the shortest NS/YM Clay
paths.  Each lane has a proof-or-kill target.  No lane may flip a Clay flag
from a receipt, finite computation, branch-count heuristic, one-loop
diagnostic, or carrier-only theorem.

## Workers

1. `W1NSResiduePhysicalStretch`
   Define `BraidResidue369(K)` as an analytic functional of `u`, `omega`,
   and `p`, then prove or kill `BraidResidueControlsPhysicalStretching`.

2. `W2NSDynamicDecayTube`
   Prove or kill deterministic `DynamicBraidResidueDecayForNS`, with
   `CoherentTubeExclusion` as the adversarial test case.

3. `W3NSLerayCarrierTransfer`
   Prove or kill Leray/supervoxel enforcement and any
   carrier-structured dense/stable transfer needed to move from carrier
   data to all smooth divergence-free data.

4. `W4YMRGMonotonicityRho`
   Prove or kill `YMNonperturbativeRGMonotonicity` and
   `ContinuumUniformRhoBound` at usable margin `rho <= 0.90`.

5. `W5YMLeakageBalaban`
   Prove or kill `ContinuumUniformLeakageBound` and the resulting
   `YMBalabanContinuumLimitWithUsableMargin`.

6. `W6YMConstructiveQFTTerminals`
   Prove or kill the downstream constructive-QFT terminals:
   Shimura-to-flat universality, self-adjoint Hamiltonian, mass-gap survival,
   OS/Wightman reconstruction, and nontrivial SU(3).

## Governance

All workers inherit the same boundary:

- NS branch counting is not deterministic NS.
- Trit signs do not control amplitudes unless an analytic bound is proved.
- One-loop YM flow is diagnostic only.
- Carrier or Shimura results do not solve flat continuum YM without a
  universality theorem.
- Clay flags remain false until terminal theorem witnesses are inhabited.
