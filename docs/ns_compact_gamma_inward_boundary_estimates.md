# Inward compact-Γ boundary estimates

This tranche refines the already-merged admissible-preservation module with theorem-facing names for the exact A1–A12 invariant-region leaves.

## What is formalised

- **A1–A2:** continuity of packet energy, compact Γ, state size, and off-packet ratio, together with lower/upper one-sided Dini derivative availability on the strong interval.
- **A3–A4:** the exact packet-energy balance interface, near-shell replenishment lower bound, and inward packet-energy derivative.
- **A5–A7:** compact-Γ near-shell gain, a quantitative tail-radius choice, absorbed far-tail control, and strict lower-Dini inwardness at the Γ floor.
- **A8–A9:** the accumulated-loss alternative, sufficient initial reserve, and the backward expenditure-margin carrier.
- **A10:** strict upper-Dini inwardness at the X-norm ceiling.
- **A11:** strict upper-Dini inwardness at the off-packet-ratio ceiling on the positive-denominator branch, plus explicit division-free continuation when the denominator vanishes.
- **A12:** composition with the existing no-first-exit owner, cutoff-uniform Galerkin preservation, and closed passage to the continuum limit.

## Proof boundary

The module proves the logical composition of named analytic certificates. It does **not** manufacture the Navier–Stokes estimates themselves. In particular, near replenishment, compact-Γ gain, uniform tail decay, Dini identities, norm dissipation, ratio control, cutoff independence, and closedness remain explicit obligations to be discharged by the corresponding analytic modules.

This keeps the lane fail-closed: importing the module cannot promote global regularity or BKM continuation without concrete inhabitants of every required certificate.
